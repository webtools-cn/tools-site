❓ FAQ

What is the NATO Phonetic Alphabet?

The NATO Phonetic Alphabet is the internationally standardized (ICAO/ITU) spelling alphabet used in radio communications. Each letter corresponds to a clear code word: A=Alpha, B=Bravo, C=Charlie... Z=Zulu. It ensures accurate letter transmission in radio communications.

What are common use cases?

Common use cases include: 1) Pilot-tower communication (aviation); 2) Military radio communications; 3) Spelling names on customer service calls; 4) Letter confirmation for voice assistants; 5) Two-way radio communication; 6) Verbal transmission of passwords or serial numbers.

What's the difference from other alphabets?

The NATO Phonetic Alphabet is the internationally recognized standard (ICAO/ITU), widely used in global aviation and military. LAPD alphabet is used by US law enforcement. APCO alphabet serves public safety communications. NATO is the most globally recognized.

Are there phonetic codes for numbers?

Numbers are typically read directly, but the NATO standard also defines pronunciation guides: 0=Zero, 1=One, 2=Two, etc. This tool preserves numbers by default.
