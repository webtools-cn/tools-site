How to Use

This online grade calculator is simple yet powerful. Here is a detailed guide:

Weighted Average Mode: Select the "Weighted Average" tab and add courses row by row. Each row requires a course name, credit hours (weight), and score. The tool automatically computes the weighted average using the formula: "Sum(Score × Credits) ÷ Sum(Credits)". You can freely add or remove course rows, and the result updates instantly. Weighted averages are commonly used for semester evaluations and scholarship assessments.

GPA Calculator Mode: Select the "GPA Calculator" tab. The tool converts scores into grade points based on your chosen standard (4.0 US scale or 5.0 China scale). Under the 4.0 scale: A=4.0, B=3.0, C=2.0, D=1.0, F=0. Under the 5.0 scale: Excellent=5.0, Good=4.0, Average=3.0, Pass=2.0, Fail=0. GPA is calculated as "Sum(Grade Point × Credits) ÷ Sum(Credits)".

Custom Standard: If your institution uses a different grading standard, switch to "Custom" mode. A "Grade Point" input column will appear for each course, allowing you to manually enter the corresponding point value. The tool will use your custom values for the weighted calculation. After calculating, click "Copy Results" to copy the summary to your clipboard for pasting into Excel or documents.

Use Cases

Grade calculation is a frequent need for students and educators. Here are typical scenarios:

Semester Grade Review: At the end of each semester, students need to calculate their weighted average and GPA to understand academic performance. By entering each course's score and credits, this tool quickly provides the weighted average, GPA, and total credits — essential data for scholarship applications and graduate school recommendations.

Scholarship Eligibility Check: Scholarships often require a minimum GPA (e.g., 3.5/4.0 or 4.0/5.0). Use this tool to verify your GPA in advance, determine if you meet the threshold, and adjust your study strategy accordingly.

Study Abroad Applications: When applying to overseas institutions, you often need to convert domestic grades to the international 4.0-scale GPA. Different countries and schools use different conversion rules. This tool provides multiple standard references to help you accurately fill out transcripts and application materials.

Extended Knowledge

Weighted Average vs. Arithmetic Mean: The arithmetic mean simply adds all scores and divides by the number of courses, ignoring credit differences. The weighted average multiplies each score by its corresponding credits, sums these products, and divides by total credits. This better reflects true academic standing because high-credit courses have a greater impact on the result. For example, a 4-credit course influences the weighted average four times more than a 1-credit course.

International GPA Differences: There is no universal GPA standard worldwide. The US commonly uses the 4.0 scale, Germany uses a 1–5 scale (lower is better), and Chinese universities use 5.0 or 4.0 scales. When applying for study abroad programs, target institutions usually provide official conversion guidelines. This tool supports common 4.0 and 5.0 scales, but always refer to your target institution's specific requirements.

Practical Significance of GPA: GPA is not just a quantitative measure of academic performance — it is widely used for graduate school rankings, scholarship evaluations, exchange program selections, and even resume screening by employers. Maintaining a strong GPA is important for both academic development and career planning.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between GPA and weighted average?

Weighted average calculates the mean of scores weighted by credit hours, typically preserving the original scale (e.g., percentage). GPA (Grade Point Average) maps scores to grade points first, then computes a weighted average of those points, resulting in a standardized value (such as 4.0 or 5.0 scale). Weighted average reflects raw performance, while GPA reflects ranked performance.

How do I convert between 4.0-scale and 5.0-scale GPA?

There is no universal conversion formula because institutions define grade boundaries differently. A rough estimate is to multiply a 5.0-scale GPA by 0.8 to approximate a 4.0-scale GPA, but this is only an approximation. This tool supports both standards separately for accurate results.

Is my data saved on a server?

No. This tool runs entirely in your browser. All grade data is processed locally on your device and never uploaded to any server. Data disappears when you refresh or close the page, so remember to save your results.

Can I import data from Excel?

The current version does not support direct Excel import. You can copy course names, credits, and scores from Excel and paste them into the corresponding input fields, one row per course. CSV import may be added in a future version.

How does the custom GPA standard work?

In GPA Calculator mode, select the "Custom" option. A "Grade Point" input column will appear for each course. You can manually enter the corresponding point value for each score (e.g., if your school maps 85–89 to 3.7). The tool will use your custom values for the weighted average calculation.

Can I export the calculation results?

Yes. Click the "Copy Results" button to copy the calculation summary (including weighted average, GPA, and total credits) to your clipboard for easy pasting into Excel, Word, or other documents. CSV export may be added in a future version.

How are retake courses handled?

Different schools handle retake grades differently. Some take the highest score, some average both attempts, and some only record pass/fail. Please follow your school's academic regulations and manually enter the grade that should be counted in this tool.

Why does my GPA differ from the official school system?

Possible reasons include: 1) Different GPA scales (4.0 vs 5.0 vs percentage mapping); 2) Some courses excluded from GPA (e.g., PE, electives); 3) Different retake policies; 4) Different rounding precision. We recommend comparing against your school's official standard line by line.
