Extended Knowledge

An equalizer changes audio frequency response by adjusting gain at different frequency bands. A 10-band graphic equalizer divides the audible range (20Hz-20kHz) into 10 bands, each independently adjustable ±12dB. The human ear is most sensitive to mid frequencies (1-4kHz) and less sensitive to low and high frequencies, so boosting lows and highs can create a more balanced perceived sound - this is the famous "smile curve" principle.

📖 FAQ

What are EQ presets?

EQ presets are pre-configured equalizer parameter sets designed for different music genres or listening scenarios. For example, a Pop preset boosts mid-high frequencies for clearer vocals, while a Rock preset enhances low and high frequencies for more powerful rhythm.

What do different EQ frequency bands affect?

Low frequencies (32-125Hz) affect bass power and thickness; low-mid (250-500Hz) affects warmth; mid (1-2kHz) affects vocal clarity; mid-high (4-8kHz) affects brightness and presence; high (16kHz) affects air and detail.

How to choose the right EQ preset?

Choose based on music genre: Pop for V-curve, Rock for smile curve, Classical for gentle boost, Electronic for bass boost, Vocal for mid boost. You can also compensate for headphone characteristics.

What formats can EQ presets be exported to?

This tool supports exporting to JSON format, Equalizer APO configuration file format, VLC equalizer preset format, and direct parameter copy.

What is the difference between parametric and graphic equalizers?

Graphic equalizers use fixed frequency bands and Q values for simple, intuitive adjustments. Parametric equalizers allow free selection of frequency, gain, and Q values for more precise control. This tool provides a 10-band graphic equalizer mode.
