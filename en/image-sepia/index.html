Frequently Asked Questions

What is a sepia filter?
A sepia filter is an image processing technique that simulates old photograph effects by converting color images to warm brownish tones. This effect originates from the sepia toning process used in 19th-century photography, giving photos a nostalgic, vintage visual style.
Can I adjust the intensity of the vintage effect?
Yes. This tool provides a 0-100% intensity slider, where 0% is the original image and 100% is full sepia effect. You can freely adjust between the original and vintage effect to find the perfect look.
Are my images uploaded to a server?
No. This tool runs entirely in your browser using the HTML5 Canvas API. Image data never leaves your device and is not uploaded to any server, ensuring your privacy.
What image formats are supported?
All browser-parseable formats: JPG/JPEG, PNG, WebP, BMP, GIF, and more. Output format can be PNG (lossless) or JPEG (smaller file size).
Will image quality decrease after applying the filter?
The filter itself does not reduce image resolution. Output as PNG for full quality preservation. JPEG output has minor compression loss at 92% quality, barely noticeable.
