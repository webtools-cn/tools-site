🎯 Use Cases

Unit converters are useful in both everyday life and professional work. When traveling abroad, convert miles to kilometers or Fahrenheit to Celsius. In cooking, convert ounces to grams or cups to milliliters. Engineers frequently convert between imperial and metric units when reading and translating international technical documents.

In IT, data storage unit conversion is especially important. Hard drive manufacturers typically label capacity using decimal (1GB = 10^9 bytes), while operating systems display using binary (1GiB = 2^30 bytes), causing users to feel their drive capacity is "missing." This tool supports both standards, helping users understand the difference.

💡 Extended Knowledge

The International System of Units (SI) is the most widely adopted measurement system globally, built on seven base units: meter, kilogram, second, ampere, kelvin, mole, and candela. Most countries (with a few exceptions like the US, Myanmar, and Liberia) have officially adopted metric units.

In data storage, the confusion between decimal and binary prefixes has a long history. The IEC established binary prefix standards in 1998: KiB (kibibyte), MiB (mebibyte), GiB (gibibyte), etc., to distinguish from KB, MB, GB, and other decimal prefixes. However, manufacturer and user habits have not fully aligned, leading to ongoing confusion.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions
What unit categories does this converter support?
It supports 6 major categories: Length (meter, kilometer, inch, foot, mile, nautical mile, etc.), Weight (kilogram, gram, pound, ounce, jin, liang, etc.), Temperature (Celsius, Fahrenheit, Kelvin, Rankine), Speed (m/s, km/h, mph, knot, Mach, etc.), Area (square meter, square kilometer, hectare, acre, square foot, etc.), and Data Storage (byte, KB, MB, GB, TB, PB and binary units).
Why is 1 GB shown as less than 1 GB on my computer?
Hard drive manufacturers use decimal (1 GB = 1,000,000,000 bytes), while operating systems use binary (1 GiB = 1,073,741,824 bytes). Therefore, a 1 TB drive shows approximately 931 GB in Windows. This is a long-standing industry convention, not a defect or capacity shortage.
How is temperature conversion calculated?
The formulas are: Celsius to Fahrenheit: F = C × 9/5 + 32; Fahrenheit to Celsius: C = (F - 32) × 5/9; Celsius to Kelvin: K = C + 273.15. Rankine is similar to Kelvin but uses Fahrenheit scale intervals. This tool automatically handles all conversions when you enter any temperature value.
Are the conversion results accurate?
Yes, all conversions use internationally recognized standard values. Most conversions are precise to 6 decimal places. Data storage conversions support both SI (decimal) and IEC (binary) standards. For scientific and engineering applications, please verify against official standards.

What is the International System of Units (SI)?

Established by the International Bureau of Weights and Measures (BIPM): The SI system defines 7 base units (meter, kilogram, second, ampere, kelvin, mole, candela). All other units derive from these. This tool uses SI-standard precise conversion factors.
