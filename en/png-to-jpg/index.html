About PNG to JPG Converter

This free online PNG to JPG converter lets you quickly convert PNG images to JPG format. All processing happens in your browser — no files are uploaded to any server, ensuring your privacy. You can adjust the JPG compression quality to find the perfect balance between file size and image quality.

Why Convert PNG to JPG?

  • Smaller file size: JPG is typically 5-10x smaller than PNG, ideal for websites and social media
  • Wider compatibility: JPG is the most universal image format, supported by all devices and software
  • Perfect for photos: JPG lossy compression makes almost no visible difference for photographic images
  • Upload requirements: Many websites and systems only accept JPG format

Features

  • 100% client-side: No server uploads, files stay on your device
  • Batch conversion: Convert multiple PNG files at once
  • Adjustable quality: JPG output quality from 10% to 100%
  • Privacy-first: Zero data transfer, fully offline processing
  • Completely free: No registration, no limits, no hidden fees

FAQ

Does this tool upload my images to a server?

Absolutely not. This converter runs entirely in your browser using HTML5 Canvas technology. Your images are never uploaded to any server — all processing is done locally on your device.

Will I lose transparent background when converting to JPG?

Yes. JPG format doesn't support transparency, so transparent areas in your PNG will become white in the converted JPG. If you need to preserve transparency, keep the PNG format.

Can I convert multiple PNG files at once?

Yes. Select multiple PNG files or drag and drop them to the upload area. The tool will batch process all files and you can download each result individually.

How do I ensure the best quality?

Use the quality slider (default 90%). Higher quality means larger file size. For most use cases, 85-95% provides an excellent balance between visual quality and file size.

What can I use the converted JPG for?

Converted JPG files can be used for websites, social media, email attachments, printing, and more. JPG is the most widely supported image format on the internet.
