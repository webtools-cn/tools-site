About This Tool

The Loan EMI Calculator helps you quickly calculate the monthly payment for amortized loans. Simply enter the loan amount, annual interest rate, and loan tenure to instantly get the monthly payment, total interest, and total repayment amount. You can also view a detailed month-by-month amortization schedule showing the principal and interest breakdown for each payment.

All calculations are performed locally in your browser. No data is uploaded to any server, protecting your financial privacy.

Use Cases

Mortgage Planning: Estimate monthly mortgage payments before buying a home to assess your repayment capacity. Adjust loan amount and tenure to find the best loan plan.

Auto Loan: Calculate monthly car loan payments, compare interest rate quotes from different banks or financial institutions, and choose the most favorable plan.

Personal Loan: Calculate monthly payments for personal consumer loans, education loans, etc., and plan your personal finances wisely.

FAQ

What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is a loan repayment method where you pay a fixed amount each month, consisting of both principal and interest. Interest portion is higher in the early months and decreases over time, while principal portion increases, but the total monthly payment remains constant.

How is EMI calculated?

The EMI formula is: EMI = P * r * (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n - 1), where P is the loan principal, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12 / 100), and n is the total number of months. This tool performs the calculation automatically.

Is my financial data uploaded?

No. This tool runs entirely in your browser. All calculations happen on your device. No data is sent to any server, and it works even without an internet connection.

What loan types are supported?

This tool uses the reducing balance (amortized) method, suitable for home loans, car loans, personal loans, education loans, and most fixed-rate installment loans. For variable rate loans, you can estimate using the current rate.

How should I enter the interest rate?

Enter the annual interest rate as a percentage. For example, enter 5.5 for 5.5% annual rate. The tool automatically converts it to a monthly rate. If you know the monthly rate, multiply by 12 to get the annual rate.
