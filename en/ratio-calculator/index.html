FAQ

What calculation modes does the Ratio Calculator support?

Three modes: 1) Simplify Ratio — reduce a ratio to its simplest form (e.g. 8:12→2:3); 2) Find Missing Value — given a:b=c:x, solve for x; 3) Ratio to Percentage — convert a part-to-whole ratio to a percentage.

How does ratio simplification work?

Ratio simplification finds the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) of both numbers, then divides both by the GCD. For example, 8 and 12 have a GCD of 4, so 8:12 simplifies to 2:3.

How do I find a missing value in a proportion?

Using the proportion property: if a:b=c:x, then x=c×b÷a. For example, 2:3=6:x gives x=6×3÷2=9. This is based on cross-multiplication: a×x=b×c.

Does it support fractions and decimals?

Yes. The tool handles integer, decimal, and fractional ratio inputs. For fractions, convert to decimal first. Results are displayed with up to 4 decimal places.

What are common use cases for ratio calculations?

Common applications include: recipe scaling, map scale conversion, model scaling, engineering proportion allocation, investment share ratios, chemical mixing ratios, and educational demonstrations.
