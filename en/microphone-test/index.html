📖 How to Use Mic Test Tool

  1. Click "Start Mic Test" — Your browser will prompt for microphone access. Click Allow.
  2. Speak into your microphone — Watch the volume level bar and waveform respond in real-time.
  3. Check results — The level bar should jump when you speak. If it doesn't move, check your mic.
  4. Stop the test — Click "Stop Test" when done to release the microphone.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is the microphone test tool free?

Yes, it's completely free to use with no registration or login required. All features are available with no usage limits.

Will my microphone data be uploaded to a server?

No. This tool runs entirely in your browser locally. All audio data stays on your device and is never uploaded to any server.

Why does the browser ask for microphone permission?

The tool needs access to your microphone to test it. Your browser will show a permission prompt — simply click Allow. If you accidentally denied it, change the permission in your browser's site settings.

How do I interpret the test results?

When you speak, the volume level bar should jump above 50%. If the bar doesn't move at all, your microphone may not be working properly. Green means normal levels, red means the audio might be too loud.

Why is my microphone not responding?

Possible reasons: 1) The mic is being used by another app (Zoom, Skype, etc.); 2) Browser permission was denied; 3) Hardware issue or the mic is muted; 4) Wrong audio input device selected in your system sound settings.
