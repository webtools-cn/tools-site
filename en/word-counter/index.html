What is the Word Counter Tool

This online word counter helps you quickly count characters, words, lines, paragraphs, and sentences in any text. Whether you're writing a paper, editing an article, posting on social media, or writing code, knowing your text length is essential. This tool supports intelligent mixed Chinese-English statistics, counts Chinese characters and English words separately, and provides reading time estimation and keyword frequency analysis — a powerful assistant for content creators, students, and editors.

Statistics Explained

  • Total Characters: Sum of all characters including letters, numbers, punctuation, spaces, and line breaks
  • Chars (No Spaces): Total characters excluding spaces and line breaks
  • Chinese Characters: Count of all Chinese characters (including Chinese punctuation)
  • Chinese (No Punct): Count of pure Chinese characters excluding punctuation
  • English Words: Number of English words separated by whitespace
  • Numbers: Count of continuous number sequences
  • Sentences: Number of sentences separated by periods, question marks, exclamation marks, etc.
  • Paragraphs: Number of paragraphs separated by blank lines
  • Lines: Total number of lines in the text
  • Punctuation: Total count of Chinese and English punctuation marks

Reading Time Estimation

Reading time is estimated based on the following average speeds:

  • Chinese silent reading: ~400-600 chars/min (using 500)
  • English silent reading: ~200-300 words/min (using 250)
  • Chinese speaking: ~250 chars/min
  • English speaking: ~150 words/min
  • Writing speed: ~50-80 chars/min (using 60)

The tool automatically weights the calculation based on the Chinese-to-English ratio in your text for a more accurate estimate.

User Guide

This tool provides comprehensive text statistical analysis suitable for writing, editing, academic research, and more. Here is a detailed guide:

Step 1: Enter your text. Paste or type the text you want to analyze in the text box. You can also click the "Load Sample" button to quickly load a sample text to experience the features. Mixed Chinese-English text is supported; the tool automatically recognizes different character types. Statistics update automatically in real time as you type or modify the text.

Step 2: View statistics. The statistics area displays metrics in card format. The three most important metrics (Total Characters, Chinese Characters, English Words) are highlighted in orange. Hover over a card to see a subtle highlight effect. All data is accurate to the individual character.

Step 3: Check reading time. Below the statistics area, three time estimates are displayed: silent reading time, speaking time, and writing time. These are calculated based on industry average speeds and are for reference only. Adjust proportionally if your reading speed is faster or slower.

Step 4: Analyze keyword frequency. The "Keyword Frequency Analysis" area automatically extracts the top 10 most frequent terms. Chinese uses n-gram analysis (2-4 character combinations), while English splits by whitespace and filters common stop words (e.g. the, is, a). Each keyword shows its count and a visual bar to help you quickly understand the article's theme and vocabulary distribution.

Step 5: Copy or clear. Click "Copy All" to copy the text to the clipboard; click "Clear" to empty the text box and reset all statistics. All operations are performed locally in the browser; text is never uploaded to any server.

Extended Knowledge

Different Word Count Metrics: Chinese "word count" can be measured in multiple ways, causing confusion. Microsoft Word counts Chinese characters + English words + number strings. "Character count (excluding spaces)" counts all non-space characters. "Chinese and Korean words" counts only Chinese characters. Academic papers typically use "character count (excluding spaces)," while the publishing industry may use pure Chinese character count (excluding punctuation). This tool provides multiple metrics to meet different needs.

Factors Affecting Reading Speed: Reading speed is affected by text difficulty (academic articles ~200 chars/min, novels ~500 chars/min), reading purpose (close reading is slower than skimming), personal habits, and text formatting. The average adult silent reading speed is about 200-300 chars/min, while trained speed readers can reach 700-1,000 chars/min. Our estimates are based on averages and may vary by individual.

Keyword Extraction & Text Mining: Keyword frequency analysis is a fundamental technique in text mining. In NLP, TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency) is commonly used to measure word importance. This tool uses a simplified n-gram model for Chinese (2-4 character sliding windows) and filters stop words for English. While not as precise as professional NLP tools, it is more than sufficient for daily writing assistance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Chinese and English word counting?

Chinese counting is character-based, where each Chinese character counts as one unit. English counting is word-based, splitting text by whitespace. This tool counts both Chinese characters and English words simultaneously, making it suitable for mixed-language content. For documents with both languages (e.g. technical docs), refer to "Total Characters" or consider both Chinese characters and English words.

How is reading time calculated?

Chinese reading speed is estimated at 500 characters per minute; English at 250 words per minute. Speaking speed is approximately 50% of silent reading speed. The tool automatically weights the estimate based on the Chinese-to-English ratio in your text. Adjust proportionally if your reading speed differs.

Is my data safe?

Absolutely. This tool is a pure frontend implementation. All statistics are computed locally in your browser. No text data is uploaded to any server. All data is cleared when you close the page. Safe for sensitive content like business documents and personal information.

How does keyword frequency analysis work?

The tool automatically extracts the most frequent terms from your text. Chinese uses an n-gram algorithm on 2-4 character combinations. English splits by whitespace and filters common stop words (e.g. the, is, in). This helps you quickly understand the article's themes and vocabulary distribution. While not a professional TF-IDF analysis, it is very practical for daily writing assistance.

What statistics are supported?

10 statistics are supported: Total Characters (with/without spaces), Chinese Characters (with/without punctuation), English Words, Numbers, Punctuation, Sentences, Paragraphs, Lines, and Reading/Speaking/Writing time estimates. Covers virtually all text statistics needs.

Can it be used for academic paper word counts?

Yes. This tool provides multiple word count metrics including pure Chinese characters (no punctuation), total characters with punctuation, and English word count. Please verify against your target journal or school's specific counting standard. Our data is for reference and quick estimation only.
