📖 What is SWOT Analysis

SWOT analysis is a classic strategic planning tool developed by Albert Humphrey at Stanford Research Institute in the 1960s. It evaluates four dimensions — Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats — helping decision-makers understand internal and external factors for better strategy formulation.

SWOT Quadrants Explained

  • Strengths (S): Internal positive factors such as core competencies, brand value, patents, talented teams
  • Weaknesses (W): Internal negative factors such as resource gaps, technical shortcomings, management issues, high costs
  • Opportunities (O): External favorable factors such as market growth, policy support, technology trends, competitive gaps
  • Threats (T): External unfavorable factors such as increased competition, regulatory restrictions, technology disruption, economic downturn

Cross-Strategies

  • SO Strategy (Strengths + Opportunities): Leverage strengths to seize opportunities — best growth strategy
  • WO Strategy (Weaknesses + Opportunities): Overcome weaknesses to capture opportunities — improvement strategy
  • ST Strategy (Strengths + Threats): Use strengths to defend against threats — defensive strategy
  • WT Strategy (Weaknesses + Threats): Minimize weaknesses and avoid threats — avoidance strategy

❓ FAQ

What is SWOT analysis?

SWOT analysis is a strategic planning tool used to evaluate Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats of a project or organization. It helps decision-makers understand internal and external factors for better strategy formulation.

How to use SWOT analysis?

Steps: 1) List internal strengths; 2) List internal weaknesses; 3) Identify external opportunities; 4) Identify external threats; 5) Develop cross-strategies (SO, WO, ST, WT strategies).

What are the four SWOT quadrants?

Four quadrants: Strengths + Opportunities = Growth strategy; Weaknesses + Opportunities = Improvement strategy; Strengths + Threats = Defense strategy; Weaknesses + Threats = Avoidance strategy.
