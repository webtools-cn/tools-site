❓ What is a Unix timestamp?
A Unix timestamp (Epoch time) is the number of seconds (or milliseconds) since January 1, 1970 (UTC). It is widely used in computer systems as a timezone-independent time representation.
❓ Does it support millisecond timestamps?
Yes. The tool automatically detects whether the input is seconds or milliseconds (≥1e12 = milliseconds) and displays both formats.
