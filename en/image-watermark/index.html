📖 How to Use

This image watermark tool is simple yet powerful. Here is a detailed guide:

Upload an image: Click the upload area or drag and drop an image onto it. The tool will load the image and display a preview on the canvas. Supported formats include JPG, PNG, WebP, GIF, BMP, and SVG. All processing happens in your browser — no data is uploaded to any server.

Choose watermark type: The tool supports two modes — text watermark and image watermark. Click the "Text Watermark" tab to enter custom text and set font size, color, and opacity. Click the "Image Watermark" tab to upload your own watermark image (e.g., a logo or signature) and adjust its size and opacity.

Set watermark position: Use the 9-grid buttons to quickly position the watermark (top-left, top-center, top-right, middle-left, center, middle-right, bottom-left, bottom-center, bottom-right). The default is bottom-right. You can also fine-tune the exact position using the horizontal and vertical offset sliders.

Advanced settings: The "Rotation" slider tilts the watermark for added anti-theft protection. The "Horizontal Gap" and "Vertical Gap" settings control how the watermark repeats across the image. When set above 0, the watermark tiles across the entire image, creating a full-coverage watermark effect that effectively prevents unauthorized use.

Download: Once satisfied, click "Download Watermarked Image" to save the processed image to your device. The tool renders at the original resolution, ensuring no quality loss.

🎯 Use Cases

Image watermarks are widely used in daily life and work. Here are some typical scenarios:

Photography copyright protection: Photographers often add watermarks containing their name, website, or contact info to prevent unauthorized use. By placing a semi-transparent watermark in a corner or tiling it across the image, you can claim ownership without severely impacting the photo's viewing experience.

E-commerce product images: Online sellers add store logos or brand watermarks to product photos to prevent competitors from stealing images and to enhance brand recognition. This tool lets you quickly apply a uniform watermark style to large batches of product images.

Social media content protection: Content creators, illustrators, and designers add personalized watermarks to their original works shared on social platforms. Text watermarks can include the author's handle or social media account, directing viewers to the original source.

Corporate documents and reports: Sensitive materials such as product design drafts, UI mockups, and report screenshots can be marked with "Internal Use Only" or "Confidential" watermarks before being shared externally. A tilted, semi-transparent text watermark across the entire image is difficult to fully remove, even via screenshots.

📚 Learn More

Digital Watermark Technology: Digital watermarking is the process of embedding identifying information into digital media (images, audio, video). It comes in two forms: visible watermarks (like those in this tool) and invisible watermarks (steganography), which hide data in the least significant bits of pixels so it cannot be seen by the human eye but can be extracted with special algorithms.

How Watermarks Protect Content: Visible watermarks work by increasing the cost of theft. While it is technically possible to remove a watermark using tools like Photoshop, this requires skill and time. For automated theft (such as web scraping), a watermarked image is significantly less valuable, serving as an effective deterrent.

Legal Context: Under copyright law, works are automatically protected from the moment of creation — no registration is required. Adding a copyright notice watermark can serve as supplementary evidence of ownership. However, the most legally powerful evidence in infringement disputes remains the original, unmodified source files and records of the creation process.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Does adding a watermark reduce image quality?

No. This tool uses HTML5 Canvas to render at the original resolution locally in your browser. Export options include PNG (lossless) and JPEG (adjustable quality), ensuring the watermarked image maintains its quality.

What image formats are supported?

JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF, BMP, and SVG are all supported. For export, you can choose PNG (lossless with transparency) or JPEG (compressed).

Are images uploaded to a server?

Absolutely not. This tool is entirely frontend-based. All image processing happens locally in your browser via Canvas. Image data never leaves your device, ensuring complete privacy.

Can I add multiple watermarks at once?

The current version supports one text watermark or one image watermark at a time. However, you can use the gap settings to repeat the watermark across the entire image, creating a full-coverage watermark effect.

Can I customize the watermark position?

Yes. The tool provides a 9-grid position preset. You can also fine-tune the position using the horizontal and vertical offset sliders.
