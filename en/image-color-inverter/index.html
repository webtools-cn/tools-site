Technical Background

Color inversion works by subtracting each pixel's RGB value from 255: new = 255 - original. For example, white (255,255,255) becomes black (0,0,0). This operation is known as "negative effect" in traditional photography. In digital image processing, it's a basic pixel-level operation with O(n) complexity where n is the pixel count.
