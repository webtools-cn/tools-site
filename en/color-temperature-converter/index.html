Technical Background

Color temperature is based on Planck's black body radiation theory, describing the color of light emitted by an ideal black body when heated. Measured in Kelvin (K), low CT is red-yellow (warm), high CT is blue-white (cool). The CIE 1931 chromaticity diagram defines the Planckian locus, and CT conversion algorithms map temperature to RGB color space. Tanner Helland's algorithm is widely used with less than 2% deviation from CIE standards in the 1000K-40000K range. Common CTs: candlelight 1800K, tungsten 2700K, noon daylight 5500K, overcast 6500K.

❓ FAQ

What is color temperature?

CT measures light source color in Kelvin. Low CT is warm yellow, high CT is cool blue-white. Daylight is ~5500K.

How is CT converted to RGB?

Based on Planck's black body radiation via CIE chromaticity. This tool uses Tanner Helland's approximation algorithm.

Common light source CTs?

Candle 1800K, tungsten 2700K, warm white LED 3000K, daylight 5500K, overcast 6500K, blue sky 10000K+.

CT in design?

Warm CT creates coziness, cool CT creates professionalism. UI designers use CT for page mood and dark mode backgrounds.

Is data uploaded?

No. All calculations run locally in your browser.
