What is Word Scrambler

The Word Scrambler is a free online text processing tool that randomly scrambles the letter order of words in text. According to research at Cambridge University, as long as the first and last letters of a word are in the correct position, the brain can still identify the word even if the middle letters are scrambled. For example, "according" might become "accodnrig", but you can still read it. This phenomenon is known as "typoglycemia".

Scramble Modes

  • Keep first and last letters: Only scramble middle letters, keeping first and last in place. The scrambled text remains readable by humans but looks jumbled. This is the default mode.
  • Full scramble: All letters are randomly rearranged, including first and last. Generates harder-to-read text, suitable for more challenging word puzzles.
  • Keep punctuation: Punctuation marks stay in their original positions; only letters are scrambled.
  • Keep case: Uppercase letter positions are preserved; only lowercase letters are scrambled among themselves.

How to Use

Step 1: Enter text. Paste or type the text you want to scramble in the input box. Supports mixed English and Chinese text.

Step 2: Choose options. Check scramble options as needed. By default, first and last letters are kept, making the scrambled text still readable — the most interesting effect.

Step 3: Scramble. Click the "🔀 Scramble Text" button and the result appears below. Each click produces a different scramble. Click "🔄 Re-scramble" for a new result.

FAQ

Q: Why can I still read scrambled text?

A: According to research at Cambridge University, as long as the first and last letters of a word are in the correct position, the brain can still identify the word even if the middle letters are scrambled. This is because humans read by recognizing whole word patterns, not letter by letter. This tool keeps first and last letters by default.

Q: Does it support Chinese?

A: Yes. For Chinese text, the tool treats each character as a unit and scrambles them. You can scramble by character (randomly rearranging each character) or by word (keeping internal word order, scrambling word positions).

Q: Can I fully randomize all letters?

A: Yes. Uncheck the "Keep first and last letters" option and all letters will be randomly scrambled, including first and last. This generates harder-to-read text, suitable for more challenging word puzzles.

Q: Is my data safe?

A: Completely safe. This tool is a pure front-end implementation. All scrambling is performed locally in your browser. Text content is never uploaded to any server. All data is cleared when you close the page.
