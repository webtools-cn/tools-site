Paper Size Fundamentals

ISO 216 Standard

ISO 216 is the international paper size standard adopted by most countries (except North America). The core design principle: each size has a √2:1 (≈1.414:1) aspect ratio, so folding preserves proportions. A0 has exactly 1 square meter area, and each subsequent size halves the area.

A, B, C Series Differences

A Series: The most common office and printing standard. A4 (210×297mm) is the most widely used paper size for daily printing and documents.

B Series: Intermediate sizes between A series, used for posters, books, and specialized printing. B5 (176×250mm) is common for books.

C Series: Designed for envelopes. C4 fits unfolded A4, C5 fits A5 or folded A4, C6 fits A6 or folded A5.

DPI and Print Quality

DPI determines print quality: 300 DPI for standard high-quality printing, 72-96 DPI for screen display, 150 DPI for newspapers, 600+ DPI for professional photo printing. Formula: pixels = mm ÷ 25.4 × DPI.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is A4 paper size?

A4 paper is the most common size in the ISO 216 standard, measuring 210 × 297 mm (about 8.27 × 11.69 inches). It's part of the A-series and is widely used for printing, copying, office documents, and everyday writing. At 300 DPI, pixel dimensions are 2480 × 3508 pixels.

What is the difference between A, B, and C series?

A-series (e.g., A4) is for general office and printing. B-series (e.g., B5) provides intermediate sizes between A-series, often used for books and posters. C-series (e.g., C4) is designed for envelopes that hold A-series paper without folding.

How do I choose the right paper size?

Choose based on use: A4 for standard documents, letters, and printing; A3 for posters, charts, and large tables; A5 for brochures and notebooks; B5 for books; C4/C5 for envelopes. For photos, common sizes include 4×6 inches (10×15cm) and 5×7 inches (13×18cm).

What DPI should I use?

DPI (Dots Per Inch) determines print quality. Use 300 DPI for high-quality printing; 72-96 DPI for screen display; 150-200 DPI for newspaper printing; 600+ DPI for high-quality photo printing. Pixel size = mm size ÷ 25.4 × DPI.

What is the area of A-series paper?

A0 paper has an area of 1 square meter (841×1189mm). A1 is 0.5 m² (594×841mm), and so on. Each step down (A0→A1) halves the area by folding the long side. This consistent ratio makes scaling between sizes perfect for printing.

What is ISO 216 standard?

ISO 216 is the international paper size standard, covering A, B, and C series. Published in 1975, it's used in most countries (except North America which uses Letter, Legal, etc.). The core principle is the √2:1 aspect ratio, maintaining proportions when folded.
