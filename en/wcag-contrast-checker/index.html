FAQ

Q1: What are the WCAG 2.1 contrast ratio requirements?

WCAG 2.1 defines two conformance levels: Level AA requires normal text contrast ≥ 4.5:1 and large text (≥18pt or 14pt bold) ≥ 3:1; Level AAA requires normal text ≥ 7:1 and large text ≥ 4.5:1. The ratio is based on relative luminance, where 1:1 is no contrast and 21:1 is maximum (black on white).

Q2: Why does my color look high contrast but fail the test?

Human eyes perceive brightness differently across colors. WCAG uses a relative luminance formula based on the sRGB color space, not simple visual judgment. For example, pure red (#FF0000) on white appears high contrast visually, but its actual ratio is only 4.03:1, failing AA for normal text because red has low luminance.

Q3: What's the difference between AA and AAA? Which should I target?

Level AA is the baseline compliance requirement — most accessibility laws (ADA, Section 508, EN 301 549) use AA as the minimum standard. Level AAA is an enhanced level providing greater accessibility, ideal for audiences with visual impairments. Generally, aim for at least AA compliance, and pursue AAA when possible.
