FAQ

Q1: What is the difference between pace and speed?

Pace is time per kilometer (min/km), speed is distance per hour (km/h). Pace 5:00/km = Speed 12 km/h. Lower pace = faster, higher speed = faster. Runners typically use pace as it directly reflects per-kilometer time.

Q2: How to estimate marathon finish time?

Based on training pace: if 10K pace is 5:00/km, marathon pace is typically 15-30 sec/km slower, i.e. 5:15-5:30/km, finish time ~3:42-3:53. Also use Riegel formula: T2 = T1 × (D2/D1)^1.06.

Q3: What is negative splitting?

Negative split means running the second half faster than the first. For example, first half marathon in 1:50, second in 1:45. This is the recommended strategy for many elite runners to avoid starting too fast and crashing later.
