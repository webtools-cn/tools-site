How to Use

  1. Select the calculation mode (Cost+Price / Cost+Markup / Cost+Margin)
  2. Enter the corresponding values
  3. Click "Calculate" to get markup, margin, price, and profit
  4. All calculations happen locally in your browser

Markup vs Margin

Markup: Profit as a percentage of cost. Formula: Markup = (Price - Cost) / Cost × 100%

Margin: Profit as a percentage of price. Formula: Margin = (Price - Cost) / Price × 100%

Example: Cost $80, Price $100. Markup = 20/80 = 25%, Margin = 20/100 = 20%. Same $20 profit, different perspectives.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why distinguish markup and margin?

Markup is used in purchasing and wholesale pricing (based on cost). Margin is used in financial reporting and retail (based on price). Confusing them leads to pricing errors. For a 50% margin, you need a 100% markup.

Q: How to convert markup to margin?

Margin = Markup / (1 + Markup). Example: 25% markup → 0.25/1.25 = 20% margin. Reverse: Markup = Margin / (1 - Margin). 20% margin → 0.2/0.8 = 25% markup.

Q: What business scenarios is this for?

E-commerce pricing, wholesale/retail, restaurant cost calculation, service pricing, import/export, and manufacturing cost analysis.

Q: How accurate are the results?

Results are displayed to 2 decimal places for commercial use. Precision meets standard business requirements.

Q: Does it work offline?

Yes. After loading, all calculations work offline since everything runs in your browser.
