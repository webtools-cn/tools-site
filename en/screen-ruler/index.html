How to Use the Screen Ruler

Process your images with the Screen Ruler in three steps:

  1. Upload Your Image
    Click the upload area or drag and drop an image file. The tool supports common formats including PNG, JPG, GIF, SVG, and WebP. Your image stays on your device at all times.
  2. Configure Image Settings
    Adjust image processing options such as dimensions, quality, filters, or effects. Preview changes in real time before applying them.
  3. Download the Processed Image
    Once you are satisfied with the result, click "Download" to save the processed image. The tool never uploads your images to any server.

FAQ

How to use the screen ruler?

Click to set a start point, then click again to set an end point. The distance between the two points is calculated in pixels. You can also switch to drag mode to measure by dragging.

What does the DPI setting do?

DPI (dots per inch) determines the conversion between pixels and physical units. Standard screens are 96 DPI, Retina displays are typically 192 or higher. Setting the correct DPI gives more accurate cm/inch conversions.

How accurate are the measurements?

Pixel measurements are precise. Physical unit accuracy depends on your DPI setting matching your actual screen. We recommend calibrating by measuring a known physical object.
