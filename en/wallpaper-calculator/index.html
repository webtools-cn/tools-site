📖 How to Use

The Wallpaper Calculator is a home improvement tool that helps you calculate exactly how many rolls of wallpaper you need, avoiding both overbuying and underbuying.

Step 1: Measure the Room

Measure the total length of all four walls (perimeter) and the ceiling height with a tape measure. If you have multiple walls, add their lengths together for the perimeter.

Step 2: Subtract Openings

Measure all door and window areas that don't need wallpaper. Add them together and enter the total in the "Door & Window Area" field. The tool automatically subtracts this from the total wall area.

Step 3: Enter Wallpaper Specs

Check the wallpaper packaging for width and roll length specifications. The most common standard is 0.53m wide and 10m per roll. Select the appropriate waste factor based on pattern complexity.

Step 4: View Results

Click "Calculate Wallpaper" to see the number of rolls needed, estimated cost, and detailed breakdown including wall area, per-roll coverage, and waste area.

💡 Calculation Details

Formula: Net area = Perimeter × Height − Door/Window area. Rolls needed = ⌈Net area × Waste factor ÷ Per-roll coverage⌉.

Waste Factor: Wallpaper installation requires pattern matching, cutting, and corner handling, which inevitably produces waste. No match: 5%, simple pattern: 10%, complex pattern: 15%, large pattern: 20%.

Recommendation: Buy 1-2 extra rolls for future repairs, as different production batches may have slight color differences.

❓ FAQ

What are standard wallpaper dimensions?

The most common wallpaper standard is 0.53m wide and 10m per roll, covering approximately 5.3m². Wide-format wallpapers (0.7m or 1.06m) are also available.

Why subtract door and window areas?

Doors and windows don't need wallpaper, so their area is subtracted to reduce waste. However, trimming around openings still produces some waste, which is included in the waste factor.

Is the result accurate?

This tool uses standard formulas for reference. Actual usage may vary based on wall condition and installation technique. We recommend buying 1-2 extra rolls.
