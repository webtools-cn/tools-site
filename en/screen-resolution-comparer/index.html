What Can the Screen Resolution Comparer Do?

This tool helps you compare different display resolutions side by side, including PPI, physical dimensions, total pixels, and more. Supports preset and custom resolutions. All calculations run locally in your browser.

Core Features

• Multi-resolution comparison: Compare 2-4 different resolution configurations simultaneously

• PPI calculation: Precisely calculate PPI based on diagonal size and pixel count

• Physical dimensions: Calculate actual screen width and height (mm/inches)

• Visual comparison: Display screen sizes proportionally

• Preset resolutions: Built-in 4K, 2K, 1080p and other common resolutions

Use Cases

Buying a monitor: Compare different sizes and resolutions to find the best configuration for your needs. Understanding PPI helps you judge if screen sharpness meets your requirements.

UI/UX design: Understand the physical size of interface elements at different resolutions, ensuring designs are clear and usable on all screens.

Gaming: Compare performance impact of different resolutions. 4K has 4x the pixels of 1080p, requiring significantly more GPU power.

Extended Knowledge

PPI and Viewing Distance: Perceived PPI depends on viewing distance. Phones used at 25cm need 300+ PPI; monitors at 60cm need ~100 PPI; TVs at 2-3m need ~50 PPI. Apple's Retina standard is based on the principle that pixels become indistinguishable at normal viewing distance.

What is screen PPI?

PPI (Pixels Per Inch) measures pixels per inch, the key metric for screen sharpness. Higher PPI means finer display.

How much sharper is 4K vs 1080p?

4K has 4x the pixels of 1080p. At the same screen size, 4K has double the PPI, producing a much sharper image.

Which matters more: size or resolution?

Both are equally important. Resolution determines pixel count, screen size determines pixel density. Consider both together.

How to choose the right resolution?

Under 24-inch: 1080p; 27-inch: 2K/1440p; 32-inch: 4K; large TVs: 4K or 8K. Viewing distance also matters.

Are results accurate?

Calculations use standard 16:9 ratio, accurate for most displays. Some displays use other ratios with slightly different results.
