💡 About Gravatar

Gravatar (Globally Recognized Avatar) is a free service operated by Automattic (the company behind WordPress). When you register an email and upload an avatar, it automatically appears on any Gravatar-supported website where you comment or post.

Gravatar avatars are identified by the MD5 hash of your email address. The system is widely supported by major platforms including WordPress, GitHub, Stack Overflow, and many more.

Gravatar supports four content ratings: G (General), PG (Parental Guidance), R (Restricted), and X (Adult), ensuring appropriate content display across different contexts.

❓ FAQ

What is Gravatar?

Gravatar is a globally recognized avatar service. Register once, and your avatar follows you across thousands of websites automatically.

How do I set up Gravatar?

Visit gravatar.com, sign up with your email, and upload an avatar. Once set up, this tool will find your avatar instantly.

Why was no avatar found?

Possible reasons: the email hasn't registered with Gravatar, the email address was entered incorrectly, or the avatar's rating is set to private.

Is my privacy protected?

All queries go directly to Gravatar's official API. Your email is MD5-hashed before transmission — the plain email is never sent. No query records are stored.
