Noise Color Knowledge

Noise "colors" are based on spectral energy distribution: white noise has equal power at all frequencies (like white light), pink noise power decreases with frequency (3dB/octave), brown noise decreases faster (6dB/octave), and blue noise is the inverse of pink (more high-frequency energy). Choose based on personal preference and use case.

What is the difference between white, pink, and brown noise?

White noise: equal power at all frequencies, like TV static, most uniform. Pink noise: more low-frequency power, like a waterfall, softer and more natural. Brown noise: even stronger lows, like distant thunder, deepest. Brown noise for sleep, white noise for focus.

Why does noise help with sleep?

Noise masks sudden environmental sounds (cars, voices) through the masking effect, creating a stable sound field that reduces the brain's alertness to sound changes. A steady noise signal helps the brain relax and promotes sleep onset.

Can noise damage hearing?

Not at reasonable volumes. Keep volume below 60 dB (normal conversation level) and limit use to 8 hours. If using headphones, keep volume even lower. Stop immediately if you experience tinnitus or discomfort.

Which noise color suits which scenario?

White noise: focused study, office masking, baby soothing. Pink noise: meditation, light sleep aid, reading. Brown noise: deep sleep, anxiety relief, low-frequency masking. Blue noise: high-frequency masking, alertness.
