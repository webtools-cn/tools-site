FAQ

What are the six trigonometric functions?

Sine (sin), Cosine (cos), Tangent (tan), Cosecant (csc=1/sin), Secant (sec=1/cos), and Cotangent (cot=1/tan). They relate angles to side ratios in right triangles.

What is the difference between degrees and radians?

Degrees divide a circle into 360 parts. Radians use the arc length, where 2π radians = 360° (a full circle). π radians = 180°.

When is tan undefined?

Tan is undefined when cos = 0, i.e., at 90° (π/2) and 270° (3π/2) in standard orientation. Similarly, csc and sec have points where they're undefined.

What is the unit circle?

The unit circle is a circle of radius 1 centered at the origin. Any angle's trig values can be read directly from its intersection point with the circle.

Where is trigonometry used?

Trigonometry is used in: navigation, engineering, physics (waves, oscillations), computer graphics (rotation, 3D rendering), astronomy, and architecture.
