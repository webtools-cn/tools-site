FAQ

What is keyword density?

Keyword density is the percentage of times a keyword appears in a text compared to the total word count. The recommended range is 1-3% for SEO.

What is the ideal keyword density for SEO?

Most SEO experts recommend 1-3%. Below 1% may not signal relevance. Above 5% risks being flagged as keyword stuffing.

What are stop words?

Stop words are common words filtered out in text analysis, such as 'the', 'a', 'and', 'is'. They don't contribute meaningfully to understanding the topic.

How to optimize keyword density?

If density is too high: 1) Use synonyms; 2) Distribute keywords across paragraphs; 3) Add more supporting content; 4) Use long-tail keywords naturally.
