How to Use

This VAT calculator is simple yet powerful, suitable for everyday shopping price comparisons, invoice verification, cross-border e-commerce pricing, and more. Here is a detailed guide:

Converting Excl. to Incl. Tax: When you know the net price of a product and need to calculate the total price including VAT, select the "Excl. → Incl. Tax" mode. Enter the net price (e.g. $100) in the amount field, choose or enter the applicable tax rate (e.g. China's standard rate of 13%), and click the "Calculate" button. The tool will instantly display the tax-inclusive total ($113) and the tax amount ($13), along with the detailed formula below.

Converting Incl. to Excl. Tax: When you see a product price that already includes tax (e.g. a shelf price in a supermarket) and need to separate the net price and tax amount, select the "Incl. → Excl. Tax" mode. Enter the tax-inclusive price, select the tax rate, and click calculate. The tool uses the formula Net Price = Incl. Price ÷ (1 + Rate) to compute the precise result.

Quick Rate Selection: The tool includes presets for mainland China (13%, 9%, 6%, 3%, 1%), the EU standard rate (20%), Japan consumption tax (10%), US sales tax (~7%), and tax-exempt (0%). Click the corresponding button to auto-fill the rate without manual entry. If you need a different rate (e.g. a specific EU country's VAT), simply type it into the rate field.

Session History: Each calculation result is automatically saved to the current session's history for easy comparison across multiple transactions. The history is cleared when you refresh the page and is never uploaded anywhere.

Use Cases

The VAT calculator has a wide range of applications in daily life and business:

Shopping and Invoice Verification: When shopping online or in-store, invoices usually list the net amount, tax rate, and tax amount separately. You can use this tool to quickly verify that the invoice calculations are correct and avoid overpaying due to merchant errors. This is especially important for large purchases such as electronics, furniture, and appliances where the tax can amount to hundreds of dollars.

Cross-Border E-Commerce: E-commerce sellers need to consider different countries' VAT/GST rates when pricing products for international markets. For example, selling to EU countries requires adding the local VAT (19% in Germany, 20% in France, 20% in the UK, etc.). This tool helps you quickly calculate the tax-inclusive selling price and the import VAT that can be reclaimed, aiding in pricing decisions.

Quick Business Estimates: SME finance staff often need quick tax estimates during daily operations. This tool serves as a supplement to professional accounting software, allowing rapid verification when opening complex systems is unnecessary. For example, when the procurement department provides a tax-inclusive quote, you can quickly convert it to a net price for budget comparison.

Personal Finance and Tax Planning: Freelancers and self-employed individuals can use this tool for quick tax burden estimates when projecting annual income. Understanding how much of your gross revenue is actually tax helps with more accurate cash flow planning and tax return preparation.

Extended Knowledge

How VAT Works: Value Added Tax (VAT) is a consumption tax levied on the value added to goods and services at each stage of production and distribution. Unlike a sales tax that is only charged at the final retail stage, VAT is collected at multiple points along the supply chain. Businesses can deduct the VAT they have paid on purchases (input tax) from the VAT they collect on sales (output tax), so the tax ultimately falls on the end consumer.

Conversion Formulas: The two core formulas are: Tax-Inclusive Price = Net Price × (1 + Rate) and Net Price = Tax-Inclusive Price ÷ (1 + Rate). The tax amount is always Tax-Inclusive Price minus Net Price. Understanding these formulas allows you to perform quick mental estimates.

Global Tax Systems Compared: Different countries use different consumption tax models. China uses VAT with standard rates of 13%, 9%, and 6%. The EU uses VAT with standard rates ranging from 17% (Luxembourg) to 27% (Hungary). The US has no federal VAT but charges state-level sales tax (4%-10%). Japan uses a consumption tax of 10%. Australia uses a 10% GST. Cross-border sellers must understand these differences.

Input Tax Deduction: In China, general VAT taxpayers can deduct input tax using special VAT invoices. Requirements include: a valid special VAT invoice, consistency between invoice details and actual transactions, and certification within the prescribed period (usually 360 days). Small-scale taxpayers use simplified calculation and cannot deduct input tax.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert between tax-inclusive and tax-exclusive prices?

Net Price = Tax-Inclusive Price ÷ (1 + Rate). For example, if the tax-inclusive price is $113 and the rate is 13%, the net price = $113 ÷ 1.13 = $100, and the tax = $13. Tax-Inclusive Price = Net Price × (1 + Rate).

What are the current VAT rates in China?

China currently has three main VAT rates for general taxpayers: 13% (goods and services), 9% (transportation, construction, real estate leasing, etc.), and 6% (modern services, financial services, etc.). Small-scale taxpayers are subject to a 3% levy rate (temporarily reduced to 1% through the end of 2027).

Is my data sent to a server?

No. This tool runs entirely in your browser. All calculations are performed locally on your device. No data is uploaded to any server, and the tool works offline.

Which countries' tax rates are supported?

The tool includes presets for China (13%, 9%, 6%), US sales tax (varies by state, ~4%-10%), EU VAT (standard rates ~19%-27%), Japan consumption tax (10%), and UK VAT (20%). You can also enter any custom tax rate.

Can I use the results for official tax filing?

This tool is for quick estimation and reference only. It should not replace professional accounting software or tax advisors. For actual tax filing, please follow your local tax authority's regulations and consult a certified accountant.

Why might my calculation differ slightly from the invoice?

Invoice tax amounts are usually calculated by multiplying the net price by the tax rate and then rounding to two decimal places. Different industries or invoicing systems may have minor differences in rounding rules, potentially causing a 1-cent discrepancy. This tool uses standard rounding and should match most invoices.

Can I calculate multiple tax rates at once?

This tool processes one tax rate at a time. If you need to calculate a total tax for items with multiple rates (e.g. buying a 13% appliance and a 9% book), calculate each separately and add the results. For complex scenarios, use professional accounting software.

Does the tool support negative amounts or refunds?

The tool currently supports positive amounts only. For returns, negative invoices, export tax rebates, or other complex scenarios, please use professional tax software and consult a tax professional.
