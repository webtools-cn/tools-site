How to Use

The online currency converter is a pure frontend tool. All calculations are done locally in your browser, no data is uploaded to any server, and it works offline.

  1. Enter Amount: Type the amount you want to convert in the "Amount" field. Decimal values are supported (e.g., 100.50).
  2. Select Source Currency: Choose the currency you currently have from the "From" dropdown (e.g., USD).
  3. Select Target Currency: Choose the currency you want to convert to from the "To" dropdown (e.g., EUR).
  4. Click Convert: Press the "Convert" button to get the result based on built-in exchange rates.
  5. Quick Swap: Click the ⇅ button to instantly swap the source and target currencies.

You can also use the "Popular" currency buttons to quickly set common target currencies. The tool remembers your last selection so you don't need to reconfigure on your next visit.

Use Cases

Travel Budget Planning: Before your international trip, convert your home currency to the destination currency to estimate daily expenses for meals, transportation, and shopping.

Cross-border E-commerce Pricing: Online sellers can quickly calculate local currency prices for foreign markets to ensure competitive yet profitable pricing.

Overseas Shopping Comparison: When browsing foreign e-commerce sites, convert prices to your local currency instantly to decide whether an item is worth importing.

Extended Knowledge

Exchange Rate Basics: An exchange rate is the ratio at which one currency can be exchanged for another. Direct quotation shows how much local currency equals one unit of foreign currency, while indirect quotation shows how much foreign currency equals one unit of local currency. This tool uses USD as the base for all calculations.

Spot vs Cash Rates: Banks typically offer different rates for spot (electronic) transfers and cash exchanges. Spot rates are better because cash handling involves transportation and storage costs. The rates shown here are reference mid-rates.

Factors Affecting Rates: Key factors include economic conditions, interest rates, inflation, trade balances, political stability, and market sentiment. Major currency fluctuations can impact global markets.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the exchange rate data accurate?

The built-in exchange rates are regularly updated but are for reference only. Actual trading rates vary by bank or forex platform and typically include bid-ask spreads and fees.

Which currencies are supported?

The tool supports 25 popular currencies including USD, CNY, EUR, JPY, GBP, HKD, CAD, AUD, SGD, CHF, KRW, THB, TWD, MXN, BRL, and more.

Is my data sent to a server?

No. All calculations are performed locally in your browser. Exchange rate data is embedded in the page, and no network requests are made.

How often are the rates updated?

Built-in rates are updated manually on a regular basis. The last update date is shown at the bottom of the page.

Can I convert multiple currencies at once?

The current version supports single conversion at a time. You can quickly switch target currencies to see results for the same amount.

Can I use the conversion result for actual forex trading?

The results are for reference only and should not be used for actual trading. Real rates fluctuate, and exchange services charge fees.
