Frequently Asked Questions

What does the headline analyzer measure?

It evaluates headline effectiveness across multiple dimensions: word count, sentiment (positive/negative/neutral), power words count, authority words, emotional marketing value (EMV), and readability grade. Provides actionable suggestions to improve click-through rates.

What makes a good headline?

A good headline typically has: 6-12 words, includes emotional trigger words and numbers, promises a clear benefit, and creates curiosity. Research shows headlines with numbers get 36% more clicks, and emotional words increase engagement by 28%.

How is the Emotional Marketing Value score calculated?

EMV score measures how emotionally engaging a headline is by analyzing the density of emotional power words. Based on decades of copywriting research and A/B testing data. Scores above 70% are considered excellent.
