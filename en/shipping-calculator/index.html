FAQ

How to estimate shipping costs?

Shipping cost estimation is typically based on package weight (actual vs. dimensional weight, whichever is greater), distance, shipping method, and carrier. This tool supports weight+distance calculation or flat-rate estimation for quick shipping cost references.

What is dimensional weight? How to calculate it?

Dimensional weight (DIM weight) is a virtual weight calculated from package dimensions. Standard formula: DIM weight (kg) = Length×Width×Height(cm) / 6000 (international) or /5000 (some couriers). Shipping charges use the greater of actual and dimensional weight.

What factors affect shipping rates?

Key factors include: 1) Package weight and dimensions; 2) Distance between origin and destination; 3) Shipping method (airfreight most expensive, ground mid, sea cheapest); 4) Delivery speed (faster = more expensive); 5) Additional services (insurance, signature confirmation); 6) Fuel surcharge fluctuations.

Does this work for all carriers?

Different carriers have different pricing strategies and rate structures. This tool provides general shipping estimates suitable for reference across most major carriers. For exact quotes, use the carrier's official calculator or contact their sales team.
