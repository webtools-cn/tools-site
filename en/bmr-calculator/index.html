❓ About BMR

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the minimum energy required to sustain life at complete rest. Understanding your BMR is the first step toward scientific weight management.

Why BMR Matters

  • Accounts for 60-75% of total daily energy expenditure
  • Foundation for science-based weight loss—never eat below BMR
  • Essential for muscle gain—eat above TDEE for muscle growth
  • Helps understand your metabolic type

Factors Affecting BMR

  • Muscle Mass: Each kg of muscle increases BMR by 13-30 cal/day
  • Age: Decreases 1-2% per decade after age 20
  • Gender: Men typically have 5-10% higher BMR than women
  • Genetics: Some people naturally have faster metabolism
  • Hormones: Thyroid function directly affects metabolic rate

⚠️ Results are estimates. Actual BMR varies by individual. Consult a registered dietitian or physician for personalized advice.
