📖 What is Wind Chill Calculator?

The Wind Chill Calculator computes the feels-like temperature based on air temperature and wind speed, helping you understand the real sensation of cold weather exposure. It supports both the NWS/Environment Canada standard formula and the legacy Siple formula, with frostbite risk assessment and protection advice.

Core Features

  • Dual Formula: Supports NWS/Canada standard and legacy Siple formula
  • Real-time Calculation: Results update instantly as you type
  • Frostbite Risk Assessment: Automatically evaluates frostbite risk level and time
  • Dual Units: Displays results in both Celsius and Fahrenheit
  • Protection Advice: Provides specific recommendations based on risk level

How to Use

  1. Enter Temperature: Input the current air temperature in Celsius (recommended: -50°C to 10°C).
  2. Enter Wind Speed: Input the current wind speed in km/h (recommended: 4.8-100km/h).
  3. Select Formula: Default uses NWS/Canada standard; switch to Siple for comparison.
  4. View Results: Wind chill index, frostbite risk, and protection advice display instantly.
  5. Copy/Download: Save results via copy or download buttons.

Background Knowledge

The wind chill concept originated from Antarctic explorer Paul Siple's 1945 research, discovering that wind significantly accelerates body heat loss. In 2001, NWS and Environment Canada jointly developed the new wind chill formula based on facial heat loss modeling and modern heat transfer theory. Formula: WC = 13.12 + 0.6215T - 11.37V^0.16 + 0.3965TV^0.16, where T is Celsius temperature and V is wind speed in km/h.

FAQ

What is Wind Chill Index?

Wind Chill Index measures the combined effect of wind and low temperature on perceived cold. Higher wind speeds accelerate body heat loss, making it feel colder than actual temperature.

What is the valid range for wind chill calculation?

The NWS/Environment Canada formula applies when temperature ≤10°C and wind speed ≥4.8km/h. Outside this range, wind chill effect is negligible.

What is the difference between wind chill and actual temperature?

Actual temperature is the thermodynamic air temperature, while wind chill is the equivalent temperature felt in wind. At -5°C with 30km/h wind, wind chill is about -13°C.

How does wind chill affect health?

Lower wind chill means higher frostbite and hypothermia risk. Below -28°C, exposed skin may frostbite in 10-30 minutes; below -40°C, 5-10 minutes.

What wind speed height should I use?

The formula uses 1.5-meter height wind speed. Weather station speeds are typically at 10 meters, about 1.5 times higher than surface-level.
