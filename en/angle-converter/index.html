❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is Angle Converter free?

Yes, completely free. All conversions happen locally in your browser.

How many degrees is 1 radian?

1 radian ≈ 57.2958 degrees. Radians are the SI standard for angle measurement, commonly used in mathematics and physics.

How do I convert degrees to radians?

Multiply degrees by π/180. For example: 180° = π rad ≈ 3.14159 rad. This tool handles the conversion automatically.

How do I convert DMS to decimal degrees?

Decimal = degrees + minutes/60 + seconds/3600. E.g., 30°30'00" = 30.5°. This tool supports both formats.

What is a gradian and where is it used?

A gradian (grad) defines a right angle as 100 grad. Used in land surveying and civil engineering in some European countries. 90° = 100 grad.

Does this tool upload my data?

No. All conversions are done locally in your browser. No data is uploaded.
