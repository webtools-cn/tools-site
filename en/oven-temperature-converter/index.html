Conversion Formulas

Celsius ↔ Fahrenheit

°F = °C × 9/5 + 32

°C = (°F − 32) × 5/9

Gas Mark ↔ °C

Gas Mark is the British oven temperature standard: °C ≈ 135 + Gas Mark × 14.5

  • Gas Mark 1 = 140°C / 275°F
  • Gas Mark 4 = 180°C / 350°F (most common)
  • Gas Mark 9 = 240°C / 475°F

FAQ

How do you convert Gas Mark to Celsius?

Gas Mark is the British oven temperature standard. Gas Mark 1 equals approximately 140°C/275°F, and each step up increases by about 25°F (~14°C). This tool automatically converts all scales — just enter one value to get all equivalents.

What is the difference between Fahrenheit and Celsius?

Celsius uses water's freezing point at 0°C and boiling point at 100°C, while Fahrenheit uses 32°F and 212°F. Conversion formulas: °F = °C × 9/5 + 32, °C = (°F − 32) × 5/9. The US uses Fahrenheit; most of Europe and Asia use Celsius.

Is the actual oven temperature the same as the set temperature?

Most home ovens have a variance of ±10-15°C. We recommend using an oven thermometer for calibration. This tool provides standard conversion values; adjust according to your oven's characteristics and recipe requirements.

What temperature should I use for baking a cake?

Most cakes bake between 160-180°C. Sponge cakes around 170°C, cheesecakes with water bath at 150-160°C, pound cakes and brownies around 175°C. Temperatures above 200°C can cause cakes to brown outside while remaining uncooked inside.
