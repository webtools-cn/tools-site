Frequently Asked Questions

What is color temperature in photography?

Color temperature refers to the warmth or coolness of light, measured in Kelvin. Warm light (lower K) has more yellow/orange tones, while cool light (higher K) has more blue tones. This tool simulates adjusting white balance.

What's the difference between Temperature and Tint?

Temperature adjusts the blue-to-yellow balance (warm vs cool). Tint adjusts the green-to-magenta balance, which helps correct color casts from different lighting conditions.

Can I use this for white balance correction?

Yes! If your photo has a color cast (too blue or too yellow), move the temperature slider in the opposite direction to neutralize it. Use tint to fix green or magenta casts.
