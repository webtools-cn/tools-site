What Can the Recipe Cost Calculator Do?

The Recipe Cost Calculator is a free online tool that helps restaurant owners and home cooks precisely calculate the cost of each dish and per-serving price. Add ingredient names, quantities, units, and unit prices — the tool automatically calculates total cost, per-serving cost, suggested price, and profit. Supports multiple measurement units with automatic data saving. All calculations are done locally in your browser.

Key Features

  • Ingredient Management: Add/remove ingredients with name, quantity, unit, and unit price
  • Auto-Calculate: Real-time calculation of total cost, per-serving cost, suggested price, and profit
  • Profit Margin: Set target profit margin to auto-calculate suggested selling price
  • Multi-Unit Support: Grams, kilograms, milliliters, liters, pieces, tablespoons, cups, pounds, ounces
  • Data Saving: Auto-saves to browser local storage for continued editing
  • Export Report: Copy or download cost analysis report

Extended Knowledge

Cost control is key to profitability in the food industry. Ingredient costs typically represent 25-35% of the selling price (food cost percentage), a fundamental rule in food service. Formula: Food Cost % = Ingredient Cost / Selling Price × 100%. For example, if ingredients cost $5 and the dish sells for $15, the food cost is 33.3%. Beyond ingredients, consider labor (20-30%), rent/utilities (10-15%), and other operating costs. A sound pricing strategy keeps food cost under 35% to ensure overall profitability.

FAQ

How to calculate per-serving cost?

Enter all ingredient names, quantities, and unit prices, set the number of servings, and the tool automatically calculates total cost divided by servings.

What units are supported?

Supports grams (g), kilograms (kg), milliliters (ml), liters (L), pieces, tablespoons, cups, pounds (lb), ounces (oz).

Is my data uploaded?

No. This tool uses pure frontend technology. All calculations are done locally in your browser.

Can I save recipes?

Yes. The tool automatically saves to browser local storage. Also supports JSON export.

How to set profit margin?

Enter the target profit margin percentage, and the tool calculates suggested price = per-serving cost × (1 + profit margin).
