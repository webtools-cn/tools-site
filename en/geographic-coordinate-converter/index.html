FAQ

What is DMS (Degrees Minutes Seconds) format?

DMS is a traditional way of expressing geographic coordinates as DD°MM'SS". One degree equals 60 minutes, one minute equals 60 seconds. For example, Beijing's latitude is 39°54'26"N.

What is DD (Decimal Degrees) format?

DD uses decimal numbers for degrees, e.g., 39.9072°. North/East are positive; South/West are negative. This format is common in digital maps and GPS devices.

How precise is the conversion?

Seconds are rounded to 3 decimal places, DD to 6 decimal places. One second of latitude ≈ 30m, so 3 decimal seconds ≈ 3cm precision — suitable for professional use.

Can I convert multiple coordinates at once?

Currently single-point conversion. For batch processing, use this tool repeatedly or contact us for custom solutions.
