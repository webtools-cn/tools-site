What Can the Online Reaction Time Test Do?

The Online Reaction Time Test is a free browser-based tool for measuring reaction speed. Through simple click tests, it precisely measures your visual reaction time with support for multiple tests with averages, history tracking, and trend analysis. Using high-precision performance.now() timing, all data is saved locally in your browser and never uploaded to any server.

Core Features

  • Precise Timing: Uses performance.now() high-precision API with microsecond accuracy
  • Multiple Tests: Supports consecutive tests with automatic best and average calculation
  • Ranking System: Provides Excellent/Good/Average/Slow ratings based on reaction time
  • History Tracking: Locally saves test history with trend chart visualization
  • Keyboard Support: Supports Space bar for reduced mouse click latency
  • Anti-Cheat: Random wait times prevent premature clicks

How to Use

1. Click the test area or press Space to start

2. Wait for the red area to turn green (random 1-5 second delay)

3. Click or press Space as fast as possible when you see green

4. View your reaction time and ranking

5. After multiple tests, check your average and trend chart

Use Cases

Case 1: Esports Training

Esports players need extremely fast reaction times. Regular testing tracks training progress. Professional players typically achieve 120-150ms.

Case 2: Driving Safety Assessment

Reaction speed directly affects driving safety. Test results help assess fitness for long-distance driving. Fatigue significantly increases reaction time.

Case 3: Scientific Research

Psychology and neuroscience research frequently requires measuring reaction time. This tool provides a convenient online testing solution.

Technical Background

Human reaction time is influenced by multiple factors: age (peaks around 20, increasing 2-5ms per decade after), gender (males average 10-20ms faster), fatigue (sleep deprivation adds 50-100ms), and attention (distractions add 30-50ms). Display refresh rate also affects test results: 60Hz displays have ~16.7ms inherent latency, 144Hz displays ~6.9ms. Keyboard testing is typically 5-10ms faster than mouse due to faster mechanical response.

What is reaction time?
Reaction time is the interval between the appearance of a stimulus and your response, typically measured in milliseconds (ms). The average human visual reaction time is about 200-250ms, and auditory reaction time is about 150-200ms. Reaction time is affected by age, attention, fatigue, and other factors.
How to improve reaction speed?
Ways to improve: 1) Get adequate sleep - fatigue significantly slows reaction time; 2) Regular exercise, especially sports requiring quick reactions like table tennis or boxing; 3) Minimize distractions and stay focused during testing; 4) Practice regularly - repeated neural pathway use speeds up responses; 5) Maintain good posture and hand positioning.
Is the test accurate?
This test uses high-precision performance.now() timing with microsecond accuracy. However, results are affected by display refresh rate (typically 60Hz = 16.7ms delay), input device latency, and browser performance. Taking multiple tests and averaging provides more accurate results.
What is a normal reaction time range?
Average adult visual reaction time: Excellent 150-200ms, Normal 200-250ms, Slow 250-350ms. Professional esports players can reach 120-150ms. Reaction speed decreases with age; over 60 averages about 250-300ms.
Is my test data uploaded?
No. All test data is saved only in your browser locally (localStorage) and never uploaded to any server. You can clear your history at any time.
