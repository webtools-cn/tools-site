FAQ

Q1: What is the difference between CGST, SGST, and IGST?

CGST (Central GST) is collected by the central government, SGST (State GST) by the state government. Both apply to intra-state transactions, each at half the GST rate. IGST (Integrated GST) applies to inter-state transactions at the full rate, collected by the central government. For 18% GST: intra-state = 9% CGST + 9% SGST, inter-state = 18% IGST.

Q2: How to convert between inclusive and exclusive prices?

Exclusive → Inclusive: Inclusive = Exclusive × (1 + GST Rate/100). Inclusive → Exclusive: Exclusive = Inclusive / (1 + GST Rate/100). GST Amount = Inclusive - Exclusive.

Q3: What are the GST rate slabs in India?

India has 5 main GST rate slabs: 0% (essentials), 5% (essential goods and services), 12% (standard rate), 18% (most goods and services), 28% (luxury and sin goods). Special rates: 0.25% (gemstones) and 3% (gold).
