📖 How to Use

This tool is a free online ID photo cropper and background color changer. No registration or installation required. All processing happens locally in your browser, and no data is uploaded to any server. Follow this step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Upload Your Photo

Click the "Choose Photo" button or drag and drop your image onto the upload area. The tool supports JPG, PNG, and WebP formats. For best results, upload the original high-resolution photo. Once uploaded, you can drag to adjust the crop area on the canvas to ensure your face is properly positioned.

Step 2: Select Photo Size

In the "Photo Size" panel on the right, choose the size you need. The tool includes presets for 1-inch (25×35mm), small 1-inch (22×32mm), 2-inch (35×49mm), small 2-inch (33×48mm), passport (33×48mm), visa (35×45mm), driver's license (22×32mm), and custom sizes. If unsure, consult the issuing authority for exact requirements.

Step 3: Change Background Color

In the "Background Color" panel, click the desired color to see the effect in real time. White is standard for passports, national IDs, and driver's licenses. Blue is common for employment cards and graduation certificates. Red is used for certain medical and insurance documents. Choose according to your specific document requirements.

Step 4: Adjust and Download

Use the toolbar buttons (Zoom In, Zoom Out, Rotate Left, Rotate Right) to fine-tune the photo display. When satisfied with the result, click the "Download Photo" button to save. The downloaded image will be in standard ID photo dimensions, ready for printing or online submission.

🎯 Use Cases

ID photos are essential in daily life and work. Almost everyone needs them at some point. This tool covers the vast majority of common requirements, helping you quickly create compliant ID photos.

Official Documents: Passports, national IDs, driver's licenses, and other government documents have strict photo requirements for size and background color. This tool provides standard size templates to ensure your photos meet official standards.

Job Applications: Resume photos and employee ID photos typically require 1-inch or 2-inch photos with blue or white backgrounds. Use this tool to quickly crop professional-looking photos that meet corporate requirements.

Exams & Certifications: Various exams (civil service, graduate school, language proficiency tests, etc.) require application photos with specific size and background requirements. Prepare them in advance with this tool to avoid last-minute stress during registration.

Visa Applications: Different countries have different visa photo size and background color requirements. This tool supports multiple visa photo sizes, allowing you to quickly generate compliant visa photos for any destination.

💡 Tips & Knowledge

Although ID photos may seem simple, there are many professional standards and best practices to keep in mind. Understanding the basics will help you produce better, more compliant photos.

DPI and Print Quality: DPI (Dots Per Inch) is a key metric for print quality. ID photos typically require 300 DPI. A 1-inch photo (25×35mm) at 300 DPI needs about 295×413 pixels. If your photo resolution is too low, it will look blurry when printed. This tool displays your photo's effective DPI to help you determine if it is print-ready.

Background Color Standards: Different documents require specific background colors with standard RGB values. White backgrounds are usually pure white #FFFFFF. Blue backgrounds in China are typically #438EDB (sky blue). Red backgrounds are usually #DE2910. This tool provides standard color values to ensure compliance.

Face Proportion: Standard ID photos require the face to occupy about two-thirds of the photo. The distance from the top of the head to the top edge should be about 3-5mm, and from the chin to the bottom edge about 7-10mm. When taking the photo, face the camera directly, keep shoulders level, maintain a natural expression, and avoid wearing glasses or jewelry.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What are the standard dimensions for passport and ID photos?

Passport photos are typically 35mm x 45mm (413 x 531 pixels at 300dpi). Standard ID photos vary by country: US passport is 2x2 inches (51x51mm), UK passport is 35x45mm, and EU standard is 35x45mm. Check your specific country's requirements before printing.

Does changing the background color affect photo quality?

No. This tool uses Canvas for pixel-level processing, only replacing the background color without compressing or altering the quality of the person in the photo. Use a high-resolution original image for the best results.

Are my uploaded photos saved on a server?

No. This tool runs entirely in your browser. All image processing happens locally on your device. No data is uploaded to any server, ensuring your privacy and security.

What photo sizes does this tool support?

This tool supports 1-inch (25x35mm), small 1-inch (22x32mm), 2-inch (35x49mm), small 2-inch (33x48mm), passport (33x48mm / 35x45mm), visa (35x45mm), driver's license (22x32mm), and custom sizes. It covers most common ID photo requirements worldwide.

What background colors are required for ID photos?

Different documents require different backgrounds: White is standard for passports, national IDs, and driver's licenses in most countries. Light blue is common for employment cards and graduation certificates. Red is used for certain medical and insurance documents. Always verify the exact requirements with the issuing authority.

My photo resolution is too low. What should I do?

Upload the original high-resolution photo if possible. If the source image is low resolution, the cropped photo may not meet printing requirements (typically 300dpi). In that case, retake the photo with a better camera or visit a professional photo studio.

Can I process multiple photos at once?

Currently, photos are processed one at a time. You can upload multiple photos sequentially, crop each one with the same size and background settings, and download them individually. Batch processing will be added in a future update.
