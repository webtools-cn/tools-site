How to Use the Pixel Art Editor

Create pixel art easily with our online editor:

Pixel Art Applications

Pixel art is widely used in game development for character and scene assets, social media avatars and emojis, retro-style art creation, digital collectible (NFT) design, and web/app icon design. 8-bit and 16-bit pixel art is especially popular in indie games and retro culture.

Pixel Art Tips

1. Start small - 8x8 or 16x16 is great for beginners; 2. Keep outlines clear with dark borders; 3. Use limited palettes - classic pixel art often uses just 16 or 32 colors; 4. Use dithering to create gradients with limited colors; 5. Study classic pixel games like Super Mario and Zelda for inspiration.

FAQ

Is the Pixel Art Editor free?

Yes, completely free. No registration or login required, all features are available directly.

Does it upload data to servers?

No. All processing happens in your browser. It works even without internet.

What export formats are supported?

PNG and SVG. PNG for social media and game assets, SVG for lossless scaling. Grid lines are removed automatically.

How to adjust canvas size?

Set width and height in the toolbar (8x8 to 64x64). Note: resizing resets the canvas, so export first.

What drawing tools are available?

Brush, eraser, fill (paint bucket), and color picker. Brush draws pixels, eraser removes them, fill colors areas, picker samples colors from the canvas.
