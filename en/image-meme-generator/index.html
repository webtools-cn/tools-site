How to Use

This online meme generator is a pure frontend tool that runs entirely in your browser. No registration or login is required—just open the page and start creating.

Step 1: Upload an Image

Click the dashed area in the center of the page or drag and drop an image directly onto it. JPG, PNG, GIF, and WebP formats are all supported. All processing happens locally in your browser; images are never uploaded to any server, ensuring complete privacy.

Step 2: Add Text

Enter your desired text in the "Top Text" and "Bottom Text" fields. The classic meme format features one line at the top and one at the bottom—for example, top: "When you realize it's Monday" and bottom: "But you don't have to work."

Step 3: Customize the Style

Use the sliders to adjust the font size (20–120 px) and the color pickers to set the text color and stroke color. The stroke ensures the text remains readable against any background. We recommend the classic white text with a black stroke for maximum impact.

Step 4: Download

Click the "Download Meme" button to save your creation as a high-quality PNG image. The output quality depends on the original image resolution, so using a high-resolution source image will yield the best results.

Background Knowledge

The Origin of Memes: The term "meme" was coined by British biologist Richard Dawkins in his 1976 book The Selfish Gene, originally referring to a unit of cultural transmission. In the internet era, a meme specifically means a piece of humorous content—typically an image with text—that spreads rapidly online. Early internet memes can be traced back to "rage comics" in the early 2000s.

Classic Meme Format: The most iconic meme format uses Impact font in white with a black stroke, centered at the top and bottom of the image. This style originated around 2007 on the "I Can Has Cheezburger?" website and has been imitated countless times since. Impact remains the go-to font for memes due to its bold, easily readable letterforms.

Image Format Recommendations: For memes, PNG is generally the best choice because it supports lossless compression and transparent backgrounds. JPG works well for photos with rich colors but introduces compression artifacts. GIF is suitable for simple animated memes but limits colors to 256. WebP is a modern format that offers smaller file sizes while maintaining high quality.

Frequently Asked Questions

What image formats are supported?

JPG, PNG, GIF, and WebP formats are supported. All processing happens locally in your browser—images are never uploaded to any server, ensuring complete privacy.

You are responsible for the copyright of the original images you upload. The tool itself does not add watermarks, and the generated images are entirely yours. You may use them for personal or commercial purposes, provided you have the legal right to use the original image.

Does it support batch meme generation?

The current version supports single-image processing. You can upload different images and add text repeatedly, downloading each meme independently. Batch processing may be added in a future update.

Why does the generated text look blurry?

For the best results, use high-resolution source images. The tool optimizes text rendering with anti-aliasing and stroke techniques. If the original image has a low resolution, scaling it up may cause blurriness.
