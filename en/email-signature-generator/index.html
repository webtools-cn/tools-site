📖 Email Signature Guide

What is an Email Signature

An email signature is a block of information appended to the end of an email message, typically containing the sender's name, title, company name, contact information, and social links. A professional email signature enhances brand image and makes it easy for recipients to contact you — an essential element in business communication.

Elements of a Professional Email Signature

  • Name: Use your full name, avoid nicknames
  • Title & Company: Indicate your role and organization
  • Contact Info: Phone, email, website
  • Social Links: LinkedIn, Twitter, and other professional accounts
  • Brand Elements: Company logo, brand colors

How to Use

  1. Fill in basic information: name, title, company
  2. Add contact details: email, phone, website
  3. Choose brand color and signature template
  4. Preview the signature
  5. Copy the HTML code and paste into your email client

Use Cases

  • Business emails: Professional signatures enhance corporate image
  • Job applications: Include LinkedIn link for recruiters
  • Team collaboration: Unified signature format for brand consistency
  • Client communication: Multiple contact methods for easy reach

📖 FAQ

What is an email signature?

An email signature is a block of information appended to the end of an email message, typically containing the sender's name, title, company name, contact information, and social links. A professional email signature enhances brand image and makes it easy for recipients to contact you.

How to set up an email signature in Gmail?

To set up a signature in Gmail: 1) Click the gear icon in the top right → Settings; 2) Find the Signature section in the General tab; 3) Click Create new signature; 4) Paste the generated HTML signature into the editor; 5) Save changes.

How to set up an email signature in Outlook?

To set up a signature in Outlook: 1) Click File → Options → Mail → Signatures; 2) Click New to create a new signature; 3) Paste the HTML signature into the editor; 4) Set as default signature; 5) Click OK to save.

What information should an email signature include?

A professional email signature should include: name, title, company name, phone number, and email address. Optional additions: company website, social links (LinkedIn/Twitter), company logo, and brand colors. Keep it concise, ideally no more than 5-6 lines.

Which email clients are the generated signatures compatible with?

The generated HTML signatures are compatible with Gmail, Outlook, Apple Mail, Thunderbird, and other major email clients. Table layouts ensure maximum compatibility, avoiding CSS3 features to ensure proper display on mobile devices.
