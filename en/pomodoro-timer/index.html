❓ FAQ

What is the Pomodoro Technique?

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the 1980s. It breaks work into 25-minute focused intervals (called "pomodoros") separated by short breaks. After completing 4 pomodoros, take a longer 15-30 minute break. This method helps maintain focus and reduce mental fatigue.

Is the Pomodoro Timer free?

Yes, completely free with no registration required. All timer functions run locally in your browser. Works offline too. No ads interfere with the core timer functionality.

Can I customize work and break durations?

Yes. The default is 25-minute work / 5-minute break, but you can adjust both work and break durations from 1-60 minutes in the settings panel to fit your personal workflow.

Will the timer work in background tabs?

Yes, JavaScript timers continue running in background tabs, though precision may decrease slightly. An alarm sound plays when a session ends. For best results, keep the tab in the foreground or allow sound permissions for the site.

About the Pomodoro Timer

The Pomodoro Timer is named after the Italian word for "tomato," inspired by the tomato-shaped kitchen timer used by the technique's creator. The core idea is to break large tasks into 25-minute focused intervals with regular breaks, improving concentration and work efficiency. This browser-based Pomodoro Timer provides a complete implementation: automatic work/break mode switching, pomodoro count tracking, and customizable durations. No software installation required — just open the page and start focusing.
