What Can the Lo-Fi Beat Generator Do?

The Lo-Fi Beat Generator is a free online tool that uses Web Audio API to synthesize Chillhop-style Lo-Fi beats in real-time. It includes four tracks — drums, bass, chords, and effects — with BPM control, reverb adjustment, preset styles, and a sleep timer. Perfect for studying, working, meditation, and relaxation. No audio files to download.

Core Features

  • Real-time Synthesis: Web Audio API generates Lo-Fi beats, no audio downloads needed
  • Four-track Mixing: Independent control of drums, bass, chords, and FX
  • BPM Control: 60-120 BPM range for different moods
  • Reverb Effect: 0-100% reverb depth for spatial atmosphere
  • Preset Styles: 6 presets including Late Night Study, Rainy Day, Morning Coffee
  • Sleep Timer: 15min/30min/1hr/2hr auto-stop

How to Use

1. Click Play to start generating Lo-Fi beats

Use Cases

Deep Study Sessions

Research shows Lo-Fi music's moderate complexity occupies the brain's "wandering channel," allowing better focus on study material. Recommended: 75-85 BPM, 40-60% reverb.

Creative Work

Lo-Fi beats' laid-back atmosphere reduces anxiety and lets thoughts flow more freely. Designers and writers often use Lo-Fi music to enter a flow state.

Meditation & Relaxation

Lower BPM to 60-70, increase reverb to 60-80%, turn off drums, keep only chords and FX for a deeply relaxing atmosphere.

Background Knowledge

Lo-Fi (Low Fidelity) music originated in the 1990s, influenced by producers like J Dilla and Nujabes. Core characteristics include: deliberately retained recording noise and tape hiss, swing rhythm (subtle drum offset), jazz chord progressions (ii-V-I-vi), and sampling techniques. Lo-Fi Hip Hop has formed a "24/7 livestream" culture on YouTube, with the most famous channel Lofi Girl having over 13 million subscribers, proving this genre's global influence.

FAQ

Do I need to download audio files?▼
No. All sounds are synthesized in real-time using Web Audio API. No audio files need to be downloaded. Just open your browser and start listening.
Which browsers are supported?▼
All modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. Web Audio API is a standard feature in all modern browsers.
Can I export the audio?▼
The current version is real-time playback only. WAV/MP3 export is planned for a future update.
Why is it called Lo-Fi?▼
Lo-Fi stands for "Low Fidelity" — deliberately keeping recording noise, distortion, and imperfections for a warm, nostalgic atmosphere, as opposed to Hi-Fi (High Fidelity).
