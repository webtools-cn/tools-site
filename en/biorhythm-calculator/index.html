📖 What is Biorhythm Calculator?

The Biorhythm Calculator computes your physical, emotional, and intellectual biorhythm curves based on your birth date. It helps you understand your cyclical patterns and plan work, study, and life accordingly.

Core Features

  • Triple Calculation: Physical (23-day), Emotional (28-day), Intellectual (33-day) cycles
  • Visual Chart: 15 days before and after at a glance
  • Critical Day Alerts: Marks days when rhythms cross zero
  • Status Assessment: Auto-detects high, low, and critical phases
  • Report Export: Copy or download calculation results

How to Use

  1. Enter Birth Date: Select your birthday in the date picker.
  2. Select Query Date: Defaults to today; change to check any date.
  3. View Values: Current rhythm values and status display automatically.
  4. View Chart: The chart shows 15 days before and after the query date.
  5. Export Results: Click copy or download to save the report.

Use Cases

Sports Planning

Athletes use physical rhythm to schedule training intensity and competitions. High phase suits intense events; low phase calls for lighter training.

Exam Scheduling

Students reference intellectual rhythm for important exams and study sessions. High phase brings sharper thinking for tackling difficult problems.

Emotional Management

Understanding emotional cycles helps regulate mood during low phases and avoid impulsive decisions. High phase is great for social activities.

Work Efficiency

Combine all three rhythms to assess overall state. Schedule important work when conditions are favorable; handle routine tasks during low periods.

Background Knowledge

Biorhythm theory was proposed by Wilhelm Fliess in the late 19th century, identifying 23-day physical and 28-day emotional cycles. Alfred Teltscher later added the 33-day intellectual cycle. Formula: Value = sin(2π × days / period) × 100. Positive = high phase, negative = low phase, near zero = critical phase. Note: This theory is scientifically controversial—use as fun reference.

FAQ

What are biorhythms?

Three cyclical rhythms: physical (23-day), emotional (28-day), and intellectual (33-day). Each cycle has high, critical, and low phases starting from birth.

What is a critical day?

When a rhythm curve crosses zero, transitioning between high and low phases. Theoretically, these days bring instability and increased error risk.

Is biorhythm scientifically proven?

Controversial. Circadian rhythms exist, but fixed 23/28/33-day cycles lack rigorous validation. Use as reference only.

How to interpret values?

Range -100 to +100. Positive = high phase, negative = low phase, near zero = critical phase.

What if all three are negative?

Theoretically all states are at their lowest. Avoid major decisions and intense activities. Don't over-worry—just reference.
