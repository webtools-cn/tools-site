About WebP to PNG Converter

This free online WebP to PNG converter lets you convert WebP images to PNG or JPG format instantly. All processing happens right in your browser — no files are uploaded to any server, ensuring your privacy and security.

Why Convert WebP to PNG?

  • Compatibility: While WebP is supported by modern browsers, many legacy systems, photo editors, and upload forms still require PNG or JPG format
  • Editing: Most image editing tools have limited WebP support — converting to PNG ensures full compatibility
  • Printing: PNG provides lossless quality that's ideal for print materials and archival
  • Transparency: WebP supports transparency but some workflows handle it better with PNG alpha channels

Features

  • 100% Client-Side: No server uploads, your files never leave your device
  • Batch Conversion: Convert multiple WebP files at once
  • High Quality: Configurable output quality for JPG (10-100%)
  • Privacy First: Zero data transmission — completely offline processing
  • Free: No sign-ups, no limits, no hidden charges

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this tool upload my images to a server?

No. This converter runs entirely in your browser using HTML5 Canvas technology. Your images are never uploaded to any server. They stay on your device throughout the entire conversion process.

What's the difference between PNG and JPG output?

PNG uses lossless compression — it preserves all image data and supports transparency, resulting in larger file sizes. JPG uses lossy compression — it creates smaller files but loses some detail and doesn't support transparency. Choose PNG for graphics, logos, and screenshots. Choose JPG for photographs and complex images where smaller file size matters.

Can I convert multiple WebP files at once?

Yes. Simply select multiple files when uploading, or drag and drop multiple files onto the upload zone. The tool will convert all of them in a batch, and you can download each individually or use the "Download All" feature.

Will the image quality be reduced after conversion?

When converting to PNG, the output is lossless — no quality loss occurs. When converting to JPG, you can adjust the quality setting (default 90%) to balance between file size and image quality. The conversion from WebP to PNG is pixel-perfect.

Is there a limit on file size or number of files?

Since processing is local, the limit depends on your device's available memory. Most images up to 50MB work well. For batch conversions with very large files, your browser's memory is the only constraint.
