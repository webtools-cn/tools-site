❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is WPM and CPM?
WPM (Words Per Minute) is the standard typing speed unit, where 1 WPM = 5 correct keystrokes. CPM (Characters Per Minute) measures characters typed correctly per minute. This tool displays both metrics.
What is a normal typing speed?
Average speeds: Beginner 20-35 WPM (80-140 CPM), Casual user 40-50 WPM (160-200 CPM), Proficient 60-80 WPM (240-320 CPM), Professional stenographers can reach 100+ WPM (400+ CPM). Programmers should aim for 50-70 WPM minimum.
How can I improve my typing speed?
5 tips: 1) Learn touch typing (don't look at keyboard) - this is most important; 2) Maintain correct hand position (ASDF-JKL;); 3) Practice 15-30 minutes daily; 4) Focus on accuracy over speed; 5) Test regularly with this tool and track your progress.
Does this typing test upload my data?
No. Everything runs entirely in your browser. Your typed text never leaves your device. All statistics (WPM, accuracy, time) are calculated locally. The tool works even offline.
How fast should a programmer type?
While typing speed is less critical for programming than problem-solving skills, 50-70 WPM is a good target for programmers to avoid keyboard input becoming a bottleneck. Focus more on accuracy and shortcuts than raw speed.
