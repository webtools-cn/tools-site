Common Speed Units

km/h: Standard speed unit used worldwide for road traffic and sports.

mph: Used primarily in the US and UK. 1 mph ≈ 1.609 km/h.

Knot: Standard for maritime and aviation. 1 knot = 1 nautical mile/h ≈ 1.852 km/h.

Mach: Speed relative to the speed of sound. 1 Mach ≈ 1,225 km/h at sea level.

m/s: The SI unit for speed. 1 m/s = 3.6 km/h.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How accurate is the converter?

The converter uses high-precision conversion factors with results shown to 6 decimal places, suitable for everyday and engineering use.

Q: Does Mach number change with altitude?

Yes. The speed of sound varies with temperature, which changes with altitude. This tool uses sea level standard temperature (15°C) at approximately 340 m/s.

Q: How do I convert km/h to mph?

Divide km/h by 1.609344. For example, 120 km/h ÷ 1.609344 ≈ 74.56 mph. Multiply mph by 1.609344 to convert back.

Q: What is the speed of light in km/h?

The speed of light is approximately 1,079,252,848.8 km/h (299,792,458 m/s). This tool supports speed of light as a unit.

Q: Does this tool upload my data?

No. All calculations happen locally in your browser. No data is uploaded to any server.

Q: What is a knot?

A knot is one nautical mile per hour. One nautical mile equals 1.852 km (one minute of latitude). Knots are used in marine and air navigation.
