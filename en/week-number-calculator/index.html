FAQ

What is ISO week number?

ISO 8601 week numbering system: weeks start on Monday, and the first week of the year is the week containing the first Thursday. January 1 may belong to the last week of the previous year, and December 31 may belong to week 1 of the next year.

How do I find the current week number?

Click the 'Today' button to auto-fill today's date, or enter any date manually. The tool instantly shows the ISO week number, week start/end dates, and week progression.

Why is January 1 in week 52 of last year?

If January 1 falls on Friday, Saturday, or Sunday, it belongs to the last week of the previous year per ISO 8601. Only Jan 1 on Monday-Thursday starts week 1 of the new year.

Does the tool handle year-crossing weeks?

Yes. The tool automatically handles weeks that cross year boundaries. For example, week 1 of 2026 may start on December 29, 2025. All calculations follow ISO 8601 strictly.

What are use cases for week numbers?

Project planning, logistics scheduling, school curriculum planning, financial reporting, manufacturing scheduling, and international business coordination all use ISO week numbers for consistent weekly planning.
