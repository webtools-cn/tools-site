What is Gematria?

Gematria (גימטריה) is an ancient method that assigns numerical values to letters and studies the resulting sums of words or phrases. Originally rooted in Hebrew tradition, it has since been extended to English and other languages.

Main methods

  • Hebrew Gematria: Aleph=1, Bet=2, ... Tav=400. Used in Jewish scriptural study.
  • English Simple (A=6): A=6, B=12, C=18... step of 6, common in Western numerology research.
  • English Pythagorean (A=9): A=1,B=2,...,I=9,J=1... cycling 1-9, the most common modern Gematria.
  • Reverse: Z=1, Y=2, ... A=26, reverse mapping for comparison.

FAQ

Is Gematria scientifically based?

Gematria is a historical and cultural study method, not a rigorous science. It's primarily used for Jewish scriptural exegesis, linguistic research, and cultural analysis. This tool is for learning and research only and does not constitute any prediction or divination advice.

What's the difference between Simple and Pythagorean?

Simple (A=6) increments by 6 (A=6,B=12,...,Z=156) and yields larger numbers; Pythagorean (A=9) cycles 1-9 (A=1,B=2,...,I=9,J=1,...) and is the most common modern English Gematria method.

How are Hebrew final letters handled?

Hebrew has 5 letters with special final forms (ך ם ן ף ץ). This tool uses the value of their regular form (e.g. final Mem ם = 40, same as regular Mem מ).

Are punctuation and spaces counted?

No. This tool only counts letter values; spaces, punctuation, digits and other non-letter characters are ignored.
