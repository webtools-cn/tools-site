What is Profit Margin?

Profit margin is a key metric that measures a company's profitability, expressed as the percentage of profit relative to revenue. Common margin metrics include gross margin, net margin, and markup rate. Gross margin reflects product profitability, while net margin reflects overall business profitability.

Profit Margin Formulas

Gross Margin = (Selling Price - Cost) / Selling Price \u00d7 100%. Markup = (Selling Price - Cost) / Cost \u00d7 100%. Net Margin = (Revenue - Cost - Expenses - Tax) / Revenue \u00d7 100%. Higher margins indicate stronger profitability.

How to Improve Profit Margins

Ways to improve margins include reducing procurement costs, optimizing production efficiency, raising prices, cutting operating expenses, and increasing the share of high-margin products. Margins vary significantly across industries, so always compare within your sector.
