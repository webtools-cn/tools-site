📖 What Does This Calculator Do?

The Driveway Cost Calculator determines the total cost of paving a driveway based on its dimensions (length × width), the selected material type, and thickness. It supports concrete, asphalt, gravel, pavers, resin-bound surfaces, and tar & chip, with optional base preparation and labor costs.

Key Features

  • Area Calculation: Automatically computes the paving area in square feet
  • Material Volume: Calculates required material volume in cubic yards
  • Cost Breakdown: Itemizes material, base prep, and labor costs
  • Combined Rate: Shows the all-in cost per square foot
  • Material Guide: Provides pros and cons for each material type

Reference Prices

  • Concrete: $8/sq ft — 30-50 year lifespan, high durability, low maintenance
  • Asphalt: $5/sq ft — 15-30 year lifespan, quick installation, needs periodic sealing
  • Gravel: $2/sq ft — 10-15 year lifespan, lowest cost, good drainage
  • Pavers: $15/sq ft — 25-40 year lifespan, attractive, individually replaceable
  • Resin-bound: $18/sq ft — 20-25 year lifespan, permeable, modern look
  • Tar & Chip: $7/sq ft — 15-25 year lifespan, economical and practical

How to Use

  1. Enter dimensions: Input the driveway length and width in feet.
  2. Select material: Choose from the dropdown menu.
  3. Enter thickness: Input paving thickness in inches (typically 4-6" for concrete).
  4. Additional costs: Optionally enter base prep and labor rates ($/sq ft).
  5. View results: Get area, material volume, cost breakdown, and total.

FAQ

How is driveway paving cost calculated?

Driveway paving cost = area × unit price. Area is length × width, and unit price depends on material type and thickness. Base preparation and labor costs are additional.

Is concrete or asphalt more cost-effective?

Asphalt has a lower initial cost, but concrete lasts longer and needs less maintenance. Long-term, concrete offers better value; for tight short-term budgets, asphalt is a good choice.

What thickness should a driveway be?

For residential driveways, 4-6 inches of concrete is standard. For heavy vehicles, use 6-8 inches. Gravel driveways need at least 2-4 inches.
