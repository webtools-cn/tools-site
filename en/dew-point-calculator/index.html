📖 What Can This Dew Point Calculator Do?

The Dew Point Calculator uses the Magnus formula to compute dew point temperature, wet bulb temperature, and absolute humidity from air temperature and relative humidity. It also assesses comfort level and condensation risk. Ideal for HVAC design, building moisture assessment, and meteorological analysis.

Key Features

  • Dew Point Temperature: Precise calculation using the Magnus formula (°C and °F)
  • Wet Bulb Temperature: Estimated for evaporative cooling assessment
  • Absolute Humidity: Calculates water vapor content in g/m³
  • Comfort Assessment: Grades perceived comfort based on dew point
  • Condensation Prediction: Input surface temperature to check for condensation risk

Background Knowledge

The Magnus formula: α=(T×17.625)/(T+243.04), then Td=(243.04×(ln(RH/100)+α))/(17.625-ln(RH/100)-α). Wet bulb temperature uses the Stull approximation. Absolute humidity: AH=216.7×(RH/100×6.112×e^(17.62×T/(T+243.04))/(T+273.15)).

FAQ

What is dew point temperature?

Dew point is the temperature at which air becomes saturated (100% RH) at constant pressure. Below this temperature, water vapor condenses into dew, fog, or clouds.

What formula is used?

The Magnus formula, valid for -40°C to 50°C, one of the most widely used empirical dew point formulas in meteorology.

How does dew point relate to comfort?

Below 10°C feels dry, 12-16°C comfortable, 16-20°C slightly humid, 20-24°C muggy, above 24°C extremely uncomfortable.

How to predict condensation?

When surface temperature is below the dew point, condensation occurs. Input surface temperature to check.

Dew point vs relative humidity?

RH changes with temperature while dew point is an absolute moisture measure. Dew point is more stable and reliable.
