📖 FAQ

What is reverb effect?

Reverb (Reverberation) is the natural acoustic effect created when sound waves reflect off surfaces in an enclosed space. When sound hits walls, ceilings, and floors, it creates numerous reflections that gradually decay, forming reverb. Reverb makes audio sound more natural and spacious, and is one of the most commonly used effects in music production and audio post-processing.

What reverb presets are available?

This tool provides multiple spatial presets: Hall - large concert hall effect with long reverb; Room - natural room effect, warm; Cathedral - church effect with very long, grand reverb; Cave - deep, echoing cave effect; Studio - professional studio short reverb; Plate - metallic plate reverb, bright and clear.

What is Dry/Wet mix?

The Dry/Wet mix controls the ratio between the original signal (dry) and the reverb signal (wet). 100% dry means only original audio, 100% wet means only reverb effect. A mix of 20%-50% is typically recommended to preserve clarity while adding natural spaciousness.

Is audio uploaded to a server?

No. All audio processing is done locally in your browser using the Web Audio API ConvolverNode. Audio data never leaves your device, ensuring privacy.

What scenarios is this tool suitable for?

Audio reverb is suitable for: 1) Adding spaciousness to dry vocal recordings; 2) Adding reverb to instruments in music production; 3) Making podcast audio sound fuller; 4) Simulating different acoustic environments for video voiceovers; 5) Creating special spatial effects for creative audio production.
