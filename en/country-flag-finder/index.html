💡 Did You Know?

A national flag is a symbol of a country's sovereignty and identity. There are 195 sovereign states recognized by the United Nations, each with its own unique flag design.

Vexillology is the scientific study of flags, including their design, history, and symbolism. Good flag designs typically follow principles of simplicity, meaningful symbolism, limited colors, and recognizability.

The world is traditionally divided into seven continents: Asia, Africa, North America, South America, Antarctica, Europe, and Oceania.

❓ FAQ

How many countries are included?

This tool includes all 195 UN-recognized sovereign states plus some de facto independent territories.

Where does the flag data come from?

Flags are displayed using Unicode Emoji standard, compatible with all modern browsers and operating systems. All data is provided locally and works offline.

Why do some flags show as letters?

Flag emojis may not display correctly on older operating systems. For best results, use Windows 10+, macOS 10.12+, iOS 10+, or Android 7+.
