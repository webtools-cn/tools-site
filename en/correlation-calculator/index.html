💡 About Pearson Correlation

Pearson correlation coefficient (r) measures the linear relationship between two variables, ranging from -1 to +1:

• |r| ≥ 0.8: Strong · |r| ≥ 0.5: Moderate · |r| ≥ 0.3: Weak · |r| < 0.3: Very weak / none

• r > 0: Positive correlation (both increase together)

• r < 0: Negative correlation (one increases as other decreases)

• r = 0: No linear correlation

Important Notes

✅ Correlation only measures linear relationships

✅ Outliers strongly influence the result

✅ Correlation does not imply causation
