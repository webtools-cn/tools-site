About EXIF Viewer

This free EXIF viewer reads metadata embedded in JPEG images, including camera model, shooting parameters (aperture, shutter speed, ISO), GPS location, capture time, and more. All processing happens in your browser — no files are uploaded to any server.

What is EXIF data?

EXIF (Exchangeable Image File Format) is a metadata standard embedded in digital photos. When you take a photo with a digital camera or smartphone, the device automatically records shooting parameters and writes them into the image file.

Why view EXIF data?

  • Learn photography: Analyze shooting settings of great photos
  • Privacy check: See if your images contain GPS location data
  • Image management: Organize photos by capture time or device
  • Forensics: Verify image source and authenticity

FAQ

What is EXIF data?

EXIF is metadata embedded in JPEG images containing camera model, capture time, aperture, shutter speed, ISO, focal length, GPS location, and more.

Does this tool upload my images?

No. All EXIF reading is done in your browser using JavaScript. Your images never leave your device.

Why do some images have no EXIF data?

Images downloaded from social media, processed by compression tools, or edited with certain software may lose EXIF data. PNG and WebP files typically don't contain EXIF metadata.

Does EXIF contain private information?

Yes. EXIF may contain capture time, device info, and GPS coordinates. Check and remove sensitive EXIF data before sharing images on social media.

Does it support PNG format?

The tool shows basic file info for PNG images. However, EXIF standard primarily applies to JPEG — most PNG images don't contain EXIF metadata.
