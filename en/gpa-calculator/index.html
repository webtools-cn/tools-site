FAQ

What is weighted GPA?

Weighted GPA (Grade Point Average) is calculated by weighting each course's grade points by its credit hours. Formula: ∑(grade points × credits) ÷ ∑(credits). Courses with higher credits have a greater impact on your overall GPA.

What's the difference between 4.0 and 5.0 scale?

The 4.0 scale is the most common international standard, with a maximum GPA of 4.0 (90%+). The 5.0 scale is used by some universities for honors courses, with a maximum GPA of 5.0 (95%+). This tool supports both scales plus percentage-to-4.0 conversion.

What information do I need?

You'll need each course's credit hours (typically 1-6) and your grade (percentage). Some schools use letter grades - you can convert them to percentages first. Check with your institution for their specific GPA calculation policy.

Is my data uploaded to a server?

No. This tool runs entirely in your browser. All calculations happen locally on your device. Your grades never leave your computer, and the tool works even when you're offline.
