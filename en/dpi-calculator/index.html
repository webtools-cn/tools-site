❓ FAQ

What is DPI and PPI?

DPI (Dots Per Inch) refers to dots per inch, typically used in printing; PPI (Pixels Per Inch) refers to pixels per inch, used for digital displays. In screen contexts, they are often used interchangeably to indicate pixel density. Higher values mean sharper displays.

How to calculate DPI/PPI?

PPI = √(horizontal pixels² + vertical pixels²) ÷ screen diagonal size (inches). For example, a 5.5-inch screen at 1920×1080, PPI = √(1920² + 1080²) ÷ 5.5 ≈ 401 PPI.

What is the maximum PPI the human eye can see?

It depends on viewing distance. At 25cm, the human eye limit is about 720 PPI; at 30cm (normal phone distance) about 600 PPI; at 50cm (desktop distance) about 350 PPI. Apple's "Retina" standard is 326 PPI at 25cm.

How many DPI do I need for printing?

Standard document printing: 150-200 DPI; photo printing: 300+ DPI; high-quality art printing: 600-1200 DPI. Print DPI and screen PPI are different concepts.

常见问题 (FAQ)

What is DPI and PPI?

DPI (Dots Per Inch) refers to dots per inch, typically used in printing; PPI (Pixels Per Inch) refers to pixels per inch, used for digital displays. In screen display, the two are often used interchangeably, both indicating pixel density.

How to calculate DPI/PPI?

PPI = √(horizontal pixels² + vertical pixels²) ÷ screen diagonal size (inches). For example, a 5.5-inch screen at 1920×1080, PPI = √(1920² + 1080²) ÷ 5.5 ≈ 401 PPI.

What do different DPI values mean?

Below 100 PPI: low density, pixels clearly visible; 100-200 PPI: medium density, common for standard monitors; 200-300 PPI: high density, common for phones; 300+ PPI: ultra-high density, Retina level, pixels indistinguishable to human eye.
