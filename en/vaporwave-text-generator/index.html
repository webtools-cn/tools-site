Extended Knowledge

Vaporwave is an internet aesthetic movement that emerged in the early 2010s, blending 80s-90s consumer culture, early internet aesthetics, and retro-futurism. The core feature of vaporwave text is converting ASCII halfwidth letters to Unicode fullwidth letters (e.g., A→Ａ), creating a wider, rounder visual effect. Fullwidth characters were originally Unicode variants designed for CJK (Chinese, Japanese, Korean) text but were given new aesthetic meaning in vaporwave culture. Zalgo text originates from internet horror culture, creating a "corrupted" effect by stacking numerous combining diacritical marks. Small Caps are Unicode small capital letter variants commonly used in academic and design contexts.

FAQ

What is vaporwave text?▼
Vaporwave text converts normal English letters to fullwidth Unicode characters, originating from 2010s vaporwave internet culture. Fullwidth letters appear wider and rounder with a retro-futuristic feel.
What is the difference between fullwidth and halfwidth?▼
Halfwidth characters are standard ASCII, each occupying 1 byte width. Fullwidth characters are Unicode wide variants occupying 2 bytes, appearing wider and rounder — the core feature of vaporwave text.
Can I use the generated text on social media?▼
Yes. The generated text uses standard Unicode characters that work on Twitter, Instagram, Discord, WeChat, and other platforms. Some platforms may display special characters differently.
What is Zalgo text?▼
Zalgo text adds numerous combining diacritical marks above and below characters, creating a 'corrupted' or 'glitchy' visual effect often used for horror or eerie expressions.
Is my data uploaded to any server?▼
No. This tool runs entirely in your browser. All text conversion is done client-side with no data uploaded to any server.
