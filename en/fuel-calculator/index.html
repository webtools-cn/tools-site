FAQ

How to calculate fuel consumption?

Formula: Fuel consumption (L/100km) = Fuel used (L) / Distance traveled (km) × 100. This tool supports entering distance, fuel consumption data, and fuel price to automatically calculate total fuel used, L/100km, and total cost.

How to estimate fuel cost for a trip?

Fuel cost = Distance(km) / 100 × L/100km × Price/L. Example: 500km, 8L/100km, $1.2/L = 500/100×8×1.2 = $48. This tool performs these calculations instantly for your trip planning.

What is normal fuel consumption?

Normal fuel consumption varies by vehicle: small cars 6-8L/100km, mid-size 8-10L/100km, SUVs 10-15L/100km, hybrids 4-6L/100km. Actual consumption may increase due to city traffic, AC use, and driving habits.

How to improve fuel efficiency?

Tips: 1) Maintain steady speed, avoid hard acceleration/braking; 2) Regular maintenance (oil changes, air filters, tire pressure); 3) Reduce vehicle weight; 4) Use AC wisely; 5) Avoid prolonged idling; 6) Use cruise control on highways.
