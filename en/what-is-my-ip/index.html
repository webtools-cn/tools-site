What Is a Public IP Address?

A public IP address is your unique identifier on the internet. Just like your home address, every device connected to the internet needs a unique IP address to communicate. IP addresses come in two formats: IPv4 and IPv6.

IPv4 vs IPv6

  • IPv4: 32-bit address (e.g., 192.168.1.1). About 4.3 billion addresses, now mostly exhausted.
  • IPv6: 128-bit address (e.g., 2001:db8::1). Virtually unlimited address space.

What Can IP Lookup Tell You?

IP lookup reveals your connection status, ISP, and approximate location (usually city-level). This helps with network troubleshooting, remote access setup, and understanding your online presence.

FAQ

Can my exact location be found from my IP?

No. IP geolocation typically only shows city-level accuracy, not your street address. For additional privacy, use a VPN or proxy service.

Why does my IP address change?

Most home broadband connections use dynamic IP assignment (DHCP). Your IP may change when you restart your router. Static IPs usually require extra payment from your ISP.

What's the difference between IPv4 and IPv6?

IPv6 is the next-generation internet protocol that solves IPv4 address exhaustion. If your network supports IPv6, you'll have both IPv4 and IPv6 addresses.

Is this tool safe?

Absolutely. This tool uses pure frontend technology. All information is displayed locally. IP data is fetched via standard API and is not stored on any server.
