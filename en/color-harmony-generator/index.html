💡 Tips & Usage
• Complementary: Colors opposite on the wheel, high contrast for emphasis
• Triadic: Three colors evenly spaced (120°), balanced and vibrant
• Analogous: Adjacent colors on the wheel, harmonious and soothing
• Split-Complementary: Base + two colors adjacent to its complement, softer contrast
• Tetradic: Two complementary pairs, rich but needs careful balancing
• Monochromatic: Same hue with varying saturation/lightness, elegant and simple

Frequently Asked Questions

What is color harmony?

Color harmony refers to the theory of combining colors in a visually pleasing way based on their positions on the color wheel. Common types include complementary, triadic, and analogous schemes.

What harmony types are supported?

Six types are supported: Complementary, Triadic, Analogous, Split-Complementary, Tetradic, and Monochromatic.

How do I copy a color value?

Click the copy button on any color card to copy its hex value to the clipboard. You can also copy all colors at once.

Is my data secure?

All color calculations run locally in your browser. No data is uploaded to any server.
