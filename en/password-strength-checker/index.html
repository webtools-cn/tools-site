Technical Details

Password Entropy: Entropy measures password unpredictability in bits. Higher entropy means exponentially more brute force attempts needed. A 12-character mixed-type password has ~70-80 bits of entropy, taking billions of years to crack.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my password sent to a server?

Absolutely not. All analysis is done locally in your browser. Nothing is transmitted over the network.

How is the score calculated?

Length (30%), character diversity (25%), entropy (20%), common password check (15%), pattern check (10%). Score range: 0-100.

What makes a strong password?

At least 12 characters; mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols; no common words or personal info. Use a password manager.

Is there a common password list?

Yes, over 10,000 common passwords built-in, checked locally. You'll get a clear warning if your password is on it.

How to improve my score?

Increase length, add more character types, avoid patterns and dictionary words, use passphrases.
